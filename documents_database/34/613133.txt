the river and the barle valley are both designated as biological site of special scientific interest on the chains above simonsbath is a reservoir known as pinkery pond it was formed in the 19th century when john knight and his son dammed the river at that point the pond s purpose is unknown but vestiges of a small canal can be seen nearby wheal eliza mine was an unsuccessful copper and iron mine on the river near simonsbath the river passes under a late medieval six arch stone landacre bridge in withypool and the tarr steps a prehistoric clapper bridge possibly dating from 1000 bc the stone slabs weigh up to 5 tons apiece according to local legend they were placed by the devil to win a bet the bridge is long and has 17 spans it has been designated by english heritage as a grade i listed building in dulverton the river is crossed by the barle bridge the river flows through the somerset wildlife trust s mounsey wood nature reserve and knaplock and north barton sssi first notified in 1954 which are within exmoor national park this is notable for the presence of rare plant and bird life
