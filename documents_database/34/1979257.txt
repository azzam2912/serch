nibs and its various member institutions advocate for the creation of business education programs that integrate internationalist skills and perspectives including the practice of foreign languages the study of comparative management techniques and the experience of working and studying abroad they also facilitate cooperation between educational institutions in different countries on the premise that this contributes to better relations between nations by deepening understanding of other cultures and social practices nibs was formed in 1993 and currently has approximately 70 member institutions in over 30 countries members of nibs recognize each other s qualifications have a common goal of collaboration and encourage participation in the following the current president of nibs is hans van oudheusden who also serves as head of international relations at the school of financial management rotterdam university of applied sciences previously the position was held by dr david gillingham who served in the role from its inception in 1993 until 2012 and by ian charles who led the organization from 2012 to 2016 since 2008 a key function of the organization has been the accreditation of internationally oriented schools of business around the world nibs accreditation is not intended to compete with long established accreditation bodies
