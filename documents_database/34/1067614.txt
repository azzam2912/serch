reid s inquiry dr beattie s essay and dr oswald s appeal 1774 priestley had strongly suggested that there was no mind body duality such a position shocked and angered many of his readers who believed that such a duality was necessary for the soul to exist in order to explain his belief more clearly priestley wrote the disquisitions which claimed that both matter and force are active and therefore that objects in the world and the mind must be made of the same substance moreover he contended that discussing the soul was impossible because it is made of a divine substance and humanity cannot access the divine he therefore denied the materialism of the soul while simultaneously claiming its existence although he buttressed his arguments with familiar scholarship and ancient authorities including scripture he was labeled an atheist and at least a dozen hostile refutations of the work were published by 1782
