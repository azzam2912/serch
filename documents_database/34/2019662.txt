the beach was called kamakura during japanese rule for its scenic beauty that highly resembles scenery in kamakura the bay encompasses a half moon shape beach for around 1 km length long due to its location close to the most northwestern point of taiwan island the area is very windy during the low tide the beach area becomes very large sometimes over 200 meters deep with shallow tide pool and many sand bars different minerals present in the rock around the beach have created a mottled rock surface where it has created primitive fish traps made of heaped landslide and broken coral where fish are stranded by the retreating tide the beach is the ideal place for cycling sightseeing relaxing sea watching sand sculpture and water sports there are also small shopping areas with caf s restaurants and small shops towards the north end of the beach the beach is accessible by bus from tamsui station of taipei metro
