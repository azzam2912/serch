the trust often conducts various fundraising activities in mysore city the volunteers involve themselves in a unique activity called paper empowerment in which they collect old newspapers from houses in mysore and sell them to raise money apart from setting up stalls and designing and selling greeting cards they also take up informal teaching at the school the school has no government recognition as a school despite its successful history because of the way it is structured not conforming to standards but it has been recognized as a charitable trust notable people like n r narayana murthy the founder of infosys have been reported to have visited the school in spark the rise competition sponsored by mahindra the trust ended up as the second runner up from ideas all over india six students were mainstreamed in may 2012 when they passed the sslc examination of karnataka state
