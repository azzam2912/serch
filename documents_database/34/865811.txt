1941 may 1991 was a zimbabwean sculptor known for his figurative works ndandarika grew up in rusape and was the son of a malawian bus driver and a shona mother his mother was artistic and occasionally worked as a model for the sculptor job kekana another rusape resident after completing primary school he attended a catholic boarding school at serima mission in the late 1950s his artistic talent was identified there by fr john groeber and cornelius manguma who trained him in drawing and woodcarving while at serima he was chosen by groeber to paint several murals inside st mary s church after leaving serima in 1959 he moved to salisbury and joined frank mcewen s workshop school in harare in 1960 initially he became one of mcewen s leading painters specializing in landscapes and witchcraft scenes due to mcewen s preference for untrained pagan artists ndandarika hid his training at serima for many years he also developed a new persona as having been the grandson of a sangoma alleging that he had undergone extensive training with him the height of ndandarika s painting career came when moma acquired his 1962 oil bushmen running from the rain ndandarika s signature
