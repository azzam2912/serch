he entered private practice in green bay wisconsin from 1848 to 1860 he was the attorney general of wisconsin from 1860 to 1862 he was a colonel in the united states army during the american civil war from 1862 to 1864 serving in the 32nd wisconsin volunteer infantry regiment he resumed private practice in kenosha wisconsin he was vice president and counsel for the chicago northwestern railroad company until 1873 howe was nominated by president ulysses s grant on december 9 1873 to a seat on the united states district court for the eastern district of wisconsin vacated by judge andrew g miller he was confirmed by the united states senate on december 11 1873 and received his commission the same day his service terminated on january 1 1875 due to his resignation following his resignation from the federal bench howe served as an attorney for the st paul minneapolis and omaha railroad in chicago illinois from 1875 to 1892 he died on january 4 1893 in boston massachusetts howe was the nephew of timothy o howe under whom he studied law
