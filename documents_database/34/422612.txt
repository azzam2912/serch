ghee hin literally means the rise of righteousness in chinese the ghee hin often fought against the hakka dominated hai san secret society ghee hin was initially dominated by the cantonese although hokkiens formed the majority by 1860 teochew hainanese hakka and foochow form smaller minorities one of the major leader of ghee hin was chin ah yam a hakka peasant from the rural dabu county guangdong their main lodge was located in lavender street which contained the ancestral tablets of important ex members before being donated to the tan tock seng hospital when it was torn down in 1892 following the suppression of secret societies ordinance the ghee hin were notorious for mass killings against the catholic hakka ethnic group in 1850 killing about 500 as well as post offices in 1876 against a new and more expensive monopoly on post and remittances the colonial government began to move towards surveillance control and finally suppression from the 1890s onwards ghee hin and hai san were the two secret societies that were involved in perak civil war in the 19th century ghee hin and hai san were always fighting against each other to compete for land houses and food the
