after poland s accession to the european union and accession to the schengen area in particular a significant number of poles estimated at over two million have emigrated primarily to the united kingdom germany france and ireland the majority of them according to the central statistical office of poland left in search of better work opportunities abroad while retaining permanent resident status in poland itself after poland joined the eu poles acquired the right to work in some eu countries while some of the members implemented transition periods the uk ireland sweden and malta allowed poles to work freely without any limitations from the start peaking in 2007 almost 2 3 million poles lived abroad mostly in western europe this has been the largest wave of economic migration of poles abroad since the polish emigration to the united states in late 19th and early 20th century which is estimated to have brought between about 1 5 million and 3 5 million poles to the united states emigration of poles relatively modest in the first decade or so after the fall of communism in 1989 increased significantly in the late 1990s with the share of emigrants in the overall polish population
