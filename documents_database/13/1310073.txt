state of connecticut the agency operates 18 correctional facilities it has its headquarters in wethersfield the correctional system in connecticut began with the old newgate prison in east granby it was an unprofitable copper mine that opened in 1705 the state began to use the tunnels as a prison during the revolutionary war in 1790 old newgate became the state prison for men and served in that capacity until 1827 when a new state prison was opened in wethersfield prior to the establishment of the modern connecticut department of correction in 1968 5 officers died in the line of duty all at the former state prison in wethersfield prior to the complete abolishment of capital punishment in 2015 the male death row was located at the northern correctional institution in 1995 the male death row moved from osborn correctional institution to northern the execution chamber was located at osborn the york correctional institution houses all female pretrial and sentenced prisoners in the state regardless of security status so a woman classified as a death row inmate would be housed in that prison
