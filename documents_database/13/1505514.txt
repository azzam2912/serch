bolles 132 u s 125 1889 was an action to recover out of pocket damages for alleged fraudulent representations in the sale of shares of mining stock the plaintiff was denied benefit of the bargain damages the case is important in contract law specifically legal remedies and compensating expectancies plaintiff richard j bolles agreed to buy from defendant lewis w smith four thousand shares of the stock at 1 50 per share the contract was completed in march 1880 by the payment of 6 000 plaintiff then alleged that defendant s representations regarding the stock were false and fraudulent and that in fact the stock was worthless plaintiff claimed furthermore that had the representations been true the stock would now be worth 10 per share and so plaintiff claimed that he had sustained damages of 40 000 chief justice fuller disagreed saying the measure of damages is not the difference between the contract price and the fair market value if the property had been properly represented the trial court should not have looked to what the plaintiff might have gained if the representations had been true but rather what he had lost by being deceived into the purchase defendant is
