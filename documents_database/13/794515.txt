in march 1812 at the age of 24 he was promoted to the rank of lieutenant colonel in the 13th u s infantry at queenston heights he was to command the regular contingent in the initial crossing of the niagara river from new york state into ontario he crossed over the canadian side of the river once a secure foothold had been established but his boat crew panicked and returned to the american side of the river and was absent when the u s troops who had crossed were cut off and forced to surrender he was blamed for the american defeat by colonel solomon van rensselaer and other officers chrystie was accused of cowardice after the incident despite this he was promoted to colonel the following year chrystie died of natural causes on 23 july 1813 and was buried in niagara falls new york chrystie street on the lower east side of manhattan is named for him
