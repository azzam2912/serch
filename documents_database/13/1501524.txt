his father was pastor of the first church of deliverance on south wabash avenue and he started singing in the family s gospel group the cobb quartet in his teens he took part in amateur boxing with his friend major lance graduated from crane high school and attended college with lance and barbara tyson he formed a short lived vocal group the floats who recorded an unreleased demo record in 1958 59 he released his first record rise sally rise coupled with i gotta right to cry written by another friend curtis mayfield on the small lucky label in 1963 and then issued two singles on the limelight label his fourth record let her love me written by billy butler produced by major lance and with the impressions on backing vocals was issued by blue rock records in late 1964 and rose to 31 on the billboard r b chart its success leading to leavill touring with dick clark s caravan of stars however follow up records on blue rock were unsuccessful and he moved to on to record in the 1960s for several other labels as a talent scout and assistant producer he worked with carl davis at both
