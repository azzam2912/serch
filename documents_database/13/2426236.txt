it is a shrub with weeping branches simple leaves and flowers with four small white petals boronia suberosa is a shrub with weeping branches up to its branches are covered with star shaped hairs when young but become very corky with age the leaves are simple elliptic to lance shaped long and wide on a petiole up to long the flowers are borne singly on a peduncle long the sepals are green egg shaped to triangular long wide and larger than the petals the petals are white long and both the sepals and petal enlarge as the fruit develops flowering occurs between february and may and the fruit is a more or less hairy capsule long and wide boronia suberosa was first formally described in 1997 by marco f duretto who published the description in the journal austrobaileya the specific epithet suberosa is a latin word meaning corky this boronia grows on sandstone rocks and cliff faces and is only known from the ja ja formation in kakadu national park this species is listed as near threatened under the territory parks and wildlife conservation act 2000
