the title is taken from the name of the heroine of the frame tale the daughter of the 17th century mughal emperor aurangzeb the work consists of four narrative poems with a connecting tale in prose the name lalla rookh or lala rukh l le rox means tulip cheeked and is an endearment frequently used in persian poetry engaged to the young king of bukhara lalla rookh goes forth to meet him but falls in love with feramorz a poet from her entourage the bulk of the work consists of four interpolated tales sung by the poet the veiled prophet of khorassan loosely based upon the story of al muqanna paradise and the peri the fire worshippers and the light of the harem when lalla rookh enters the palace of her bridegroom she swoons away but revives at the sound of a familiar voice she awakes with rapture to find that the poet she loves is none other than the king to whom she is engaged lalla rookh was the basis of number of musical settings including a cantata by frederic clay w g wills 1877 featuring the famous song i ll sing thee songs of araby it is also
