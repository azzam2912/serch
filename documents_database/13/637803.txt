mcgill february 12 1879may 14 1963 was an american politician who served as a united states senator from kansas from 1930 to 1939 he was a member of the democratic party born in lucas county iowa he moved with his parents to kansas when he was 5 he graduated from central normal college in great bend kansas at the turn of the century and was admitted to the state bar two years later mcgill then moved to wichita kansas where he was made deputy county attorney and then county attorney for sedgwick county kansas he was elected to the united states senate on november 4 1930 to replace charles curtis who resigned to become vice president of the united states former governor henry j allen was appointed to fill the seat until a successor was elected in the senate he was the chairman of the committee on pensions and was particularly involved in the agricultural adjustment act he was reelected in 1932 but lost reelection bids in 1938 1942 1948 and 1954 mcgill was appointed by president franklin d roosevelt as a member of the u s tariff commission a post he held until 1954 he died in st francis
