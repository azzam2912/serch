the bakery is known for its guapple pie a combination of guava and apple pie and several other local delicacies and sweets around the 1920s cesar lacson locsin and his sisters julianita nitang locsin gamboa included started baking breads and cookies in their lone brick oven within the confines of their two storey home during the glory days of silay el ideal served to cater to the gastronomic needs of gamblers who were often glued to their favorite pastime then time came when the sisters had to leave the bakeshop to the hands of locsin as the sole owner locsin gamboa on her part had to focus on raising a family and had to sell her share to her brother locsin took on the responsibility of running el ideal and eventually passed the management to his daughter alice locsin villanueva in the 1950s locsin villanueva developed el ideal by installing another brick oven adding a restaurant that occupied the whole ground floor of the ancestral house and expanding its product line that included refreshments now locsin s granddaughter maritess villanueva sanchez with her husband henry sanchez and son mark took over the business but el ideal was not without challenges
