single payer describes the mechanism by which healthcare is paid for by a single public authority not a private authority nor a mix of both single payer healthcare systems pay for all covered healthcare related services by a single government or government related source it is a strategy employed by governments to achieve several goals including universal healthcare decreased economic burden of health care and improved health outcomes for the population universal health care worldwide was established as a goal of the world health organization in 2010 and adopted by the united nations general assembly in 2015 for the 2030 agenda for sustainable development a single payer health system establishes one health risk pool consisting of the entire population of a geographic or political region it also establishes one set of rules for services offered reimbursement rates drug prices and minimum standards for required services in wealthy nations that kind of publicly managed insurance is typically extended to all citizens and legal residents examples include the united kingdom s national health service australia s medicare canada s medicare and taiwan s national health insurance single payer was coined in the 1990s to characterize the difference between the canadian health care
