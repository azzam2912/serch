representative from new york born in schenectady new york yates completed preparatory studies and was graduated from union college at schenectady in 1802 he studied law he was admitted to the bar in 1805 and commenced practice in schenectady he served in the war of 1812 under gen wade hampton on the northern frontier and was subsequently appointed aide de camp to gov daniel d tompkins yates was elected as a democratic republican to the fourteenth congress march 4 1815 march 3 1817 he served as chairman of the committee on expenditures in the department of state fourteenth congress he did not seek renomination in 1816 yates aided in the construction of the welland canal he moved to chittenango new york in 1816 where he later founded the yates polytechnic institute in 1825 he was first judge of the madison county court from 1833 until his death yates was a member of the new york state assembly madison co in 1836 and died in chittenango new york on july 10 1836 he was interred in walnut grove cemetery near chittenango new york
