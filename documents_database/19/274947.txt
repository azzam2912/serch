he was a member of the legislative assembly of manitoba from 1988 to 1993 and a cabinet minister in the government of gary filmon from 1988 to 1992 neufeld began a public practice as a chartered accountant in 1954 and was a member of the chartered accountants of manitoba he first ran for the manitoba legislature in the 1986 provincial election as a progressive conservative losing to incumbent new democrat vic schroeder by 527 votes in the north end winnipeg riding of rossmere in the 1988 election with ndp support falling throughout the province he was able to defeat schroeder by 526 votes in a rematch neufeld was appointed minister of energy and mines with responsibility for the manitoba hydro act and seniors on may 9 1988 he was relieved of the latter responsibility on april 21 1989 he was re elected by an increased margin in the 1990 provincial election neufeld stepped down from his cabinet position on january 14 1992 and resigned from the legislature on may 12 1993 there are suggestions that neufeld resigned as a matter of principle disturbed by the dubious methods used by the filmon government to conceal its deficit spending a lifelong mennonite
