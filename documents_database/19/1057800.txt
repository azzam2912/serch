bayerische reserve division was a unit of the royal bavarian army part of the german army in world war i the division was formed on mobilization of the german army in august 1914 as part of i royal bavarian reserve corps the division was disbanded in 1919 during the demobilization of the german army after world war i the division was raised and recruited in bavaria as a reserve division it included many recalled reservists and war volunteers the 1st bavarian reserve division fought in the opening phases of the war in the battle of the frontiers it then participated in the race to the sea including the battle of arras in october 1914 it remained in the trenchlines in flanders until 1916 when it entered the battle of the somme in august 1916 in april 1917 the division now redeployed to the vimy sector participated in the battle of vimy ridge in late 1917 it participated in the battle of passchendaele in 1918 the division fought in the battle of the lys the division ended the war still fighting in the lys region allied intelligence rated the division as a good division in 1917 but third class in 1918
