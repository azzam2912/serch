dalman suggested identifying the site with kefar uzziel mentioned in rabbinical sources and home to one of the 24 priestly families that settled in the galilee from the 2nd century ce onward owing to the similar sounding name of the nearby arab village uzeir believed to be a corruption of uzziel the town was abandoned in the late 4th century a synagogue one of the largest in the galilee 25 x 14 meters was built at the end of the 3rd century or the beginning of the 4th century the building featured two rows of columns an aramaic inscription in the floor mosaic reads remembered well bar tanhum who made this pavement of mosaic and roof may he be blessed amen selah a second inscription reads yo ezer the hazan and simeon his brother made this gate of the lord of heaven
