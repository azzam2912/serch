born in paris in 1851 he died in saint briac sur mer in 1944 he was originally trained as a jeweller but the 1871 franco prussian war forced him to become a librarian this proved to be the start of his enamelling career as after reading claudius popelin s history of enamelling grandhomme became fascinated by the technique after studying with auguste mollard an enameller who was experienced in the chemistry of enamelling and jules cl ment chaplain he worked with pierre puvis de chavannes and jules lie delaunay and was influenced by his friend and fellow artist rapha l collin in 1874 he presented work at the salon of french artists salon des artistes fran ais an enamel portrait of vittoria colonna in 1877 he began to collaborate with his pupil alfred jean garnier and together they presented further work at the salon des artistes fran ais including a virgin in the style of carlo crivelli and a portrait of edward vii together they won the gold medal at the 1889 universal exhibition in paris they were also the last enamellers to collaborate with gustave moreau another frequent collaborator of grandhomme s was jules brateau a silversmith and metal
