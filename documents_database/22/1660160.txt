abingdon bowls club is located in the park at the northern entrance is a monument to prince albert 1819 1861 the husband of queen victoria 48 feet high and designed by john gibbs of oxford and erected in 1865 albert park was formerly conduit field from 1860 the park together with the surrounding victorian and edwardian houses were laid out by christ s hospital of abingdon architects who worked on the estate included edwin dolby 1838 1900 his pupil j g timothy west 1860 1931 and a b west 1885 1957 as well as john gibbs who designed the 1865 stone monument to prince albert in the park this memorial was built by charles selby park crescent runs along the west north and east sides of the park in a large crescent shape around the park a spur of park crescent continues to the north the straight park road is to the south of the park and also continues to the east and the west conduit road runs south from the southeastern corner of albert park it is named after the water conduit that used to run in line with the current road this conduit carried spring water from albert
