prior to purchasing the property the real estate agents cox and coxon ltd misrepresented the 1994 pear sales as being 12 000 when it was later discovered to be only 8 801 16 as a result the leipsts sued the real estate agents for damages under the fair trading act for expected damages of 27 233 64 which was calculated being the annual loss of 3 198 84 for 20 years using a discount rate of 10 the district court made no award of damages but on appeal to the high court they were awarded 16 000 cox and coxon appealed claiming that expectation losses under contract could not be awarded under the fair trading act and instead could only calculate damages under tort the fta did not clarify the issue the court sitting as a bench of 5 ruled that under the fta damages could only be awarded under tort and not under contract
