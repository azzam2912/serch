it has up to six dark green leaves with a network of silvery veins and up to four hairy brownish and white flowers anoectochilus yatesiae is a tuberous perennial herb with up to six leaves forming a rosette on the end of its fleshy rhizome the leaves are dark green broadly egg shaped to heart shaped with a network of silvery veins long and wide up to four brownish flowers long and wide are borne on a flowering stem tall the dorsal sepal is long wide and with the petals forms a hood over the column the lateral sepals are a similar length to the dorsal sepal but narrower and spread apart from each other the petals are white glabrous long and about wide the labellum is upright white long wide and has about twelve crooked white hairs long on the narrow section the nectary spur is about long flowering occurs from july to september anoectochilus yatesiae was first formally described in 1907 by frederick manson bailey from specimens collected near kuranda and the description was published in the queensland agricultural journal the specific epithet yatesiae honours mrs arthur yates one of the collectors of the type specimen the marbled
