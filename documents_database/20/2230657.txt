during the interwar period new local muslim institutions such as the muslim community of albania arose that severed ties with the ottoman caliphate and placed a focus on localising islam in albania the albanian state also played a significant role in that process through state interference and pressuring the uptake of reforms by those institutions and wider muslim society measures that were adopted were banning the veil and others which were interpreted as modernising albania these events caused tensions within parts of muslim society between conservatives and those who viewed themselves as progressives which caused discussions and reflections about the future role of islam in albania and albanian identity the interwar era also saw sufi islam expand in albania with various orders gaining new adherents with the largest the bektashi order moving its world headquarters to albania on the eve of the balkan wars and due to the albanian revolts of 1908 and 1912 the ottomans were unsure about whether muslim albanians would defend the sovereignty of the state if war with its orthodox balkan neighbours broke out on october 1912 with bulgarian montenegrin serbian and greek armies commencing hostilities with the ottomans and entering areas populated by muslim albanians
