cancer is a group of diseases that involve abnormal increases in the number of cells with the potential to invade or spread to other parts of the body not all tumors or lumps are cancerous benign tumors are not classified as being cancer because they do not spread to other parts of the body there are over 100 different known cancers that affect humans cancers are often described by the body part that they originated in however some body parts contain multiple types of tissue so for greater precision cancers are additionally classified by the type of cell that the tumor cells originated from these types include cancers are usually named using carcinoma sarcoma or blastoma as a suffix with the latin or greek word for the organ or tissue of origin as the root for example cancers of the liver parenchyma arising from malignant epithelial cells is called hepatocarcinoma while a malignancy arising from primitive liver precursor cells is called a hepatoblastoma and a cancer arising from fat cells is called a liposarcoma for some common cancers the english organ name is used for example the most common type of breast cancer is called ductal carcinoma of the breast
