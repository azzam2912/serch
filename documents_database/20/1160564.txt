it is recorded in the national heritage list for england as a designated grade ii listed building the church was closed for worship in november 1995 and since 1996 has been used as an indoor climbing centre from the mid 1960s to the mid 1980s the church was heated by steam from the then adjacent tetley walker s brewery a new church also dedicated to st ann was built on a different site half a mile away in 2000 the church was built between 1866 and 1868 to a design by john douglas there were delays caused by bad weather and it was not until local solicitor william beamont paid the builder that the church was consecrated on 27 february 1869 in 1996 it became a climbing centre with a mezzanine in the chancel these changes are said to be reversible the church is built in red brick with some dressings in blue brick and it has a slate roof its plan consists of a six bay nave without aisles an apsidal chancel north and south porches a north vestry and a southeast tower the tower is in the angle between the nave and the chancel and in three stages
