2 c 2 during the reign of king charles ii it was passed by what became known as the habeas corpus parliament to define and strengthen the ancient prerogative writ of habeas corpus which required a court to examine the lawfulness of a prisoner s detention and thus prevent unlawful or arbitrary imprisonment the act is often wrongly described as the origin of the writ of habeas corpus but the writ of habeas corpus had existed in various forms in england for at least five centuries before and is thought to have originated in the 12th century assize of clarendon it was guaranteed but not created by magna carta in 1215 whose article 39 reads no freeman shall be taken or imprisoned or disseised or exiled or in any way destroyed nor will we go upon him nor will we send upon him except upon the lawful judgement of his peers or the law of the land the act of 1679 followed an earlier habeas corpus act of 1640 which established that the command of the king or the privy council was no answer to a petition of habeas corpus further habeas corpus acts were passed by the british parliament
