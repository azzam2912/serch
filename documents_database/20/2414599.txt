it highlights the intersections of antisemitism and ableism in world war ii era switzerland liselotte rosenberg was born in giessen germany on 15 september 1923 to clara and siegfried rosenberg a couple of jewish apple wine producers and merchants who lived and worked in the nearby town of usingen as an infant she contracted bone tuberculosis and at the age of 3 her family sent her to a sanatorium in the french speaking village of leysin in the swiss alps where auguste rollier had developed a therapy based on sunlight treatment after liselotte s family became unable to pay for her medical expenses in switzerland due to restrictions on jews being able to transfer funds abroad dr rollier allowed for her to remain in the sanatorium after her recovery she worked there as a nursing assistant before moving in 1945 to zurich to train as a social worker when liselotte last visited her parents in usingen in 1937 she became aware that nazism had a strong following in the area after suffering anti semitic abuse she was sent back to switzerland and soon after her parents fled to frankfurt germany before taking refuge in the united states in 1941 liselotte
