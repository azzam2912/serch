situated in downtown sofia it was designed in neo renaissance style by konstantin jovanovi a serbian bulgarian architect who received his education in vienna and switzerland and whose other works include the parliament of serbia building it was constructed between 1884 and 1886 by friederich wilhelm gustav liebe a young builder from saxony who was only 22 years old when construction began the building was originally painted in an off white hue but since the latter part of the 20th century has been white the building is depicted on the reverse of the bulgarian 20 leva banknote issued in 1999 and 2007 due to insufficient space in the main building at parliament square some administrative offices of the national assembly are now housed by the former headquarters of the bulgarian communist party located at the largo there has been a proposal that the entire national assembly be permanently moved to the old party house building with its inner courtyard being converted into an interior space for the plenary chamber
