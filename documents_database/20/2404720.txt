no name has been officially adopted yet for this peak it is situated at the head of fryatt creek valley on the same high ridge as mount christie brussels peak and mount lowell the nearest higher peak is karpathos peak mount olympus to the west the mountain was named in 1936 for the two kings of persia xerxes i and xerxes ii the first ascent of the mountain was made in 1936 by mr and mrs a w kramer and a mckay xerxes peak is composed of sedimentary rock laid down from the precambrian to jurassic periods then pushed east and over the top of younger rock during the laramide orogeny based on the k ppen climate classification xerxes peak is located in a subarctic climate with long cold snowy winters and short mild summers temperatures can drop below 20 c with wind chill factors below 30 c precipitation runoff from xerxes peak drains into fryatt creek and lick creek both tributaries of the athabasca river
