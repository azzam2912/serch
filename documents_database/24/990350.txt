arrosticini rustelle or arrustelle in the local dialects also known as spiedini or spidducci are typically made from castrated sheep s meat mutton or lamb cut in chunks and pierced by a skewer it is cooked on a brazier with a typically elongated shape called canala as it resembles a gutter there are two main kinds of arrosticini those made industrially consisting of cubical chunks of meat with a side of 1 cm 1 2 5 inches on skewers with a maximum length of 10 cm 4 inches and those made by hand for which the meat is cut with a knife in chunks of different sizes alternated on the skewer with pieces of ovine fat doing so provides more tenderness and a more pleasant smell this kind of arrosticini requires meat of very high quality because it has to be cooked for a longer time arrosticini originate from the food consumed by shepherds and other inhabitants of the mountainous areas in abruzzo within the villages of castilenti civitella casanova carpineto and villa celiera who were accustomed to eating even less refined food than hard sheep meat arrosticini are often accompanied by slices of traditionally home made bread soaked in
