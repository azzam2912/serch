in 1932 he was awarded a four year scholarship to the eastman school of music b m and m m where he studied with bernard rogers and howard hanson in the late 1930s he also studied briefly with ildebrando pizzetti jean sibelius and aaron copland after heading the composition departments of the st louis institute of music the kansas city conservatory of music and the cleveland institute of music read became composer in residence and professor of composition at the school of music at boston university he remained in this post until his retirement in 1978 his symphony no 1 op 30 1937 premiered by sir john barbirolli won first prize at the new york philharmonic symphony society s american composers contest while his second symphony op 45 1943 won first prize in the paderewski fund competition another first prize came in the 1986 national association of teachers of singing art song competition won by his nocturnal visions op 145 he wrote one opera villon in 1967 his book music notation a manual of modern practice 1969 1979 attempted to catalogue the rapidly changing landscape of notation for contemporary western art music
