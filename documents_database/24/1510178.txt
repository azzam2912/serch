a wooden sluice or aboiteau plural aboiteaux is then built into the dyke with a hinged door clapper valve that swings open at low tide to allow fresh water to drain from the farmland but swings shut at high tide to prevent salt water from inundating the fields aboiteau farming is intimately linked with the story of french acadian colonization of the shores of canada s bay of fundy in the 17th and 18th centuries in the kamouraska region of the st lawrence valley of quebec aboiteau diking of salt marshes was closely tied to the modernization of agriculture in the 19th and early 20th centuries the acadians constructed earthen dykes to isolate areas of salt marsh from repeated inundation by the tides a rare original aboiteau is the jewel of the west pubnico acadian museums artifacts in 1990 local residents found a couple of boards sticking out of an eroding beach on double island west pubnico they returned to the site in 1996 to remove the aboiteau to preserve and display it at the museum
