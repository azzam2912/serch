warmed by the kuroshio current the village has a warmer and wetter climate than central tokyo mikurajima village was founded on october 1 1923 when the izu islands were administratively divided into villages and town the village economy is dominated by seasonal tourism supplemented by forestry and commercial fishing there is also some small scale farming tourists come for sports fishing and scuba diving due to its difficulty of access it receives considerably fewer visitors than the other islands in the izu chain due to the low population and limited number of visitors the natural habitat remains relatively untainted electric power on to the village is provided by a small hydroelectric power plant mikurajima has no major harbor apart from the dolphin tours access to the island is limited to the t kai kisen ferry that sails from miyakejima and helicopter to hachij jima izu shima and miyakejima the village operates a public elementary and middle school
