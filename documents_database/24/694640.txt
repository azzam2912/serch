the play focuses on the trials and tribulations of an irishman an englishman and an american edward michael and adam who are kidnapped and held hostage by unseen arabs in lebanon as the three men strive for survival they also strive to overcome their personal and nationalistic differences related to this is each individual s own attempt to maintain sanity under the watchful eye of both captors and supposed comrades at times the dramatic dialogue reaches a level of beckettian absurdity as even the audience is unable to draw a distinction between the characters insanity and humour we are made witness and accomplice to a humour based on something apparently ghastly the loss of rationality the play was first staged in july 1992 at the hampstead theatre london and transferred to the west end vaudeville theatre september october 1992 it was produced on broadway at the booth theatre opening on 29 may 1992 and closing on 13 june 1993 after 292 performances and 2 previews directed by robin lefevre it starred stephen rea edward james mcdaniel adam and alec mccowen michael michael york took the michael role late in the play s run it received tony award nominations for best
