it consists of the entrance area of a former tidal inlet which during the middle ages connected the north sea with the ports of sluis and bruges inland the zwin inlet was formed originally by a storm that broke through the flemish coast in 1134 creating a tidal channel that reached some 15 km inland and was also connected through another channel to the mouth of the scheldt further north east the new waterway offered access to the sea to the inland city of bruges which consequently rose to become one of the foremost medieval port cities of europe the towns of damme sluis and sint anna ter muiden were also located on the zwin however from the late 13th century onwards the channel was affected by progressive silting which ultimately caused the waterway to become unusable and cut off the harbour of bruges from the sea the present day nature reserve was founded in 1952 it has an area of 1 25 square kilometres in knokke heist belgium and 0 33 square kilometres in sluis netherlands it is famous for its large variety in salt resistant flora such as sea lavender it is also popular with bird watchers there
