it has a single tubular leaf and up to twenty pale green tan and lilac coloured flowers it was first collected in the 1960s in a location known as the hundred of koppio on the eyre peninsula but was not described until 2008 from a new collection in 2004 prasophyllum laxum is a terrestrial perennial deciduous herb with an underground tuber and a single loosely erect tube shaped leaf up to long and wide the flowering stem emerges from the leaf above the ground between five and twenty strongly scented flowers are irregularly arranged along a drooping flowering stem up to long the flowers are pale green on the inside tan coloured on the outside and as with others in the genus are inverted so that the labellum is above the column rather than below it the ovary is oval shaped erect long and wide the dorsal sepal is egg shaped to lance shaped long about wide and points downwards the lateral sepals are a similar size to the dorsal sepal linear to lance shaped and free from each other the petals are linear in shape about long wide and pale green with white edges the labellum is egg shaped
