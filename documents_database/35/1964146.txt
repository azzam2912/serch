after the first world war the local burma corporation ltd took over operations and built an electrified underground railway at tiger camp and 100 hopper cars were ordered from the american pressed steel car company in the 1930s the line experienced its peak traffic steam was largely replaced in the 1970s and 1980s but today two steam locos remain in working order in present day the coal for the smelters and the finished products is transported by road ore transport from bawdwin to namtu is still done by rail the section from namyao to namtu sees little traffic namtu is a security area and restrictions are placed on visiting the railway mines and smelters except on official tours
