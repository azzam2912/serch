the legislative power in the city is vested in the unicameral bia ystok city council which has 28 members council members are elected directly every four years one of whom is the mayor or president of bia ystok like most legislative bodies the city council divides itself into committees which have the oversight of various functions of the city government bills passed by a simple majority are sent to the mayor who may sign them into law if the mayor vetoes a bill the council has 30 days to override the veto by a two thirds majority vote the current president of bia ystok elected for his first term in 2006 is tadeusz truskolaski it is also the seat of government for the podlaskie voivodeship the city is represented by several members of both houses of the polish parliament sejm and senat from the bia ystok constituency bia ystok is represented by the podlaskie and warmian masurian constituency of the european parliament the last municipal elections were won by civic platform which holds a majority of the seats in the city council the current city mayor tadeusz truskolaski won the elections as the civic platform s candidate however he has
