after a period of being laid in reserve she served as part of the west africa squadron off africa from 1845 until 1858 on 1 january 1856 hecate sank the american brigantin chatsworth which was engaged in the slave trade her crew survived later that month she ran aground at lagos the steamship puffin was subsequently wrecked during operations to salvage her guns after being fitted out for survey operations she was assigned to the pacific station in 1860 undertaking surveys along the british columbia coast the hecate strait between the british columbia mainland and the islands of haida gwaii is named for her arriving at the australia station in 1863 she undertook survey work in botany bay moreton bay the brisbane river and torres strait before leaving the australia station in 1864 she was paid off and sold to castle of charlton for breaking in 1865
