he adopted puritan views and after being ordained without subscription was appointed to the small curacy of whitmore in staffordshire he was soon deprived by john bridgeman the high church bishop of chester who put him to much suffering he became a schoolmaster and earned a wide and high reputation for his scholarship and piety he died on 20 october 1640 the most popular of his numerous works was a short catechisme containing all the principal grounds of religion 14 editions before 1632 his treatise of faith 1632 and friendly trial of the grounds tending to separation 1640 the latter of which defines his position with regard to the church are also valuable his a tryall of the new church way in new england and old written in 1637 but published in 1644 was a reply to the responses of the new england puritans to nine questions which he had posed to them concerning the constitution and doctrine of their churches his treatise of the covenant of grace 1645 was also published posthumously
