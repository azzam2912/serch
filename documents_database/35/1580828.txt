the chief law officer the attorney general is responsible for prosecuting offenders however as a government minister the attorney general will conventionally not involve himself in individual cases instead the work of prosecution has been delegated to the crown law office headed by the solicitor general who is a senior civil servant rather than a politician the crown law office among other duties supervises the prosecution of major criminal offences much of the prosecution work itself is performed by the crown solicitors 16 senior lawyers in private law firms each appointed for a particular district and lawyers working for them crown prosecutors appear for all prosecutions in the high court and those in the district court where the defendant has elected trial by jury in most other cases the government is often represented by police prosecutors or by prosecutors working for the government department administering the law involved for example cases involving tax are often prosecuted by lawyers working for the inland revenue department
