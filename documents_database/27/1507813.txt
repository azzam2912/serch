she married guy benton johnson a sociologist and together they had two sons guy benton jr and edward she died at the age of 89 on june 12 1989 she attended baylor college for women and began studying journalism after marriage she and her husband moved from texas to take up positions at the university of north carolina at chapel hill there she was offered an associate professorship and earned her phd in history because so few women were active historians at the time johnson s first mention in the american historical review referred to her as he she published several studies of the ante bellum south delving into race relations religion freed slaves women s life and other aspects which had previously been only lightly treated her award winning book ante bellum north carolina a social history is still considered an important resource she became involved in women s organizations and issues after the end of the second world war when opportunities for women again became limited she and her husband also collaborated on several research projects
