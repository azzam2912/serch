it has been suggested that holbein is also referring to the lais who was the lover of apelles the great painter of antiquity holbein was called apelles in humanist circles the model the same used for holbein s and for his has been identified as magdalena offenburg who may have been holbein s mistress holbein s lais was painted a year or two after venus and amor and in effect acts as its companion piece though holbein does not appear to have originally planned the second painting according to some commentators the portrayal of the sitter as a hetaera or courtesan may contain a coded message by holbein about his relationship with her art historian peter claussen however dismisses this interpretation as pure nonsense both paintings employ the same colours and depict the same costume and drapery holbein adopts the style of leonardo and the lombard muralists whose work he may have studied during a visit to italy he uses leonardo s sfumato smoky technique to blend the skin tones as well as the device of the parapet to set the subject back from the viewer
