he sailed across the atlantic aboard the queen elizabeth and joined no 619 squadron raf in june 1943 he transferred to the united states army air forces usaaf as a first lieutenant with corresponding pay rise equivalent to an raf group captain but insisted on completing his tour with his crew and was seconded back to the raf remaining with no 619 until january 1944 he and his crew then volunteered to join no 617 squadron raf the dambusters and took part in various raids including operation taxable operation paravane and the attacks on the german battleship tirpitz he was awarded the distinguished service order on 17 january 1944 the commonwealth distinguished flying cross the us distinguished flying cross and five air medals after the war he was a teacher for 25 years and worked as a peace corps volunteer in nigeria for two years he never married
