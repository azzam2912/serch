representative from kansas born in mantua ohio moore attended the common schools and the western reserve eclectic institute hiram ohio he moved to lawrence kansas in 1858 he studied law and one month after his admission to the bar enlisted in the union army in the second regiment kansas volunteer infantry on may 14 1861 and served continuously until june 30 1865 when he was mustered out of the service as lieutenant colonel of the fourth regiment arkansas volunteer cavalry as major of the eighteenth and colonel of the nineteenth regiments of kansas cavalry served against the indians on the plains in 1867 and 1868 he again engaged in the practice of law and later from 1886 to 1892 engaged in the wholesale grocery business in trinidad colorado treasurer of douglas county kansas in 1886 and 1887 he successfully contested as a democrat the election of edward h funston to the fifty third congress and served from august 2 1894 until march 3 1895 he was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1894 to the fifty fourth congress he served as vice president of a national bank in lawrence kansas until his death on may 1 1914 he was interred
