the word is generally held to be a portmanteau a contraction of blob and object the origin of the term is disputed but it is often attributed to either the designer author steven skov holt or the designer karim rashid author and design journalist phil patton attributed the word to holt in 1993 in esquire magazine holt has defined a blobject as most often a colorful mass produced plastic based emotionally engaging consumer product with a curvilinear flowing shape this fluid and curvaceous form is the blobject s most distinctive feature rashid the contemporary designer who wrote the book i want to change the world was an early leader in creating blobjects and has become one of the most celebrated designers of his generation blobjects can also be found in most areas of contemporary visual culture common materials used in fabricating blobjects are plastic especially polycarbonate polypropylene or polyethylene metal and rubber with the aim being to give a more organic and animate feel the blobject trend has largely been driven by advances in computer aided design information visualization rapid prototyping materials and injection molding these technologies have given designers the chance to use new shapes and to explore transparency
