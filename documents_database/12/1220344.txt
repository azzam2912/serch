at political level it was an agreement to oppose the policies of earl of argyll who controlled the dominant political faction in scotland the most prominent pledger was the earl of montrose others included lord fleming the earl of marischal and lord almond the earl of argyll learnt of the bonds existence from lord almond and on 13 april 1641 it was discussed by the scottish committee of estates although many members of the committee were eager to press treason charges against the pledgers after montrose and others appeared before them and testified to the bonds existence and tried to justify it no action beyond having the bond burnt on a written statement extracted from the pledgers that no evil had been intended was taken lord almond who had been appointed lieutenant general of the scottish army before the committee s meeting retained his position as the covenanters did not want to emphasize division in their ranks
