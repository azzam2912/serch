public parks and nature reserves cover a large area in rome and the city has one of the largest areas of green space amongst european capitals the most notable part of this green space is represented by the large number of villas and landscaped gardens created by the italian aristocracy while many villas were destroyed during the building boom of the late 19th century a great many remain the most notable of these are villa borghese villa ada and villa doria pamphili the lamian gardens latin horti lamiani were a set of gardens located on the top of the esquiline hill in rome in the area around the present piazza vittorio emanuele they were based on the gardens of the consul aelius lamia a friend of tiberius and soon by the time of caligula became subsumed into the imperial property the horti liciniani were a set of gardens in ancient rome which originally belonged to the gens licinia in the third century a d they were the property of the emperor gallienus himself a member of that gens the gardens were probably on the esquiline hill at the top of which gallienus erected a colossal statue of himself the 4th
