the stealth part of the term is that business will often use miscellaneous fees to charge customers without the customers even knowing the fees existed as they were hidden in the fine print of a signed a contract the inflation part of the term relates to the up charging of the service without actually providing anything additional since most companies charge a fee to accept payment a portion gets built into profit and revenue a big example of stealth inflation can be overdraft fees from banks surcharges from telco providers processing fees and installation fees another form of stealth inflation is devaluation of money by a government sometimes in order to pay off debt with lower value currency the unintended higher cost of imported goods and raw material is passed on to the consumer yet not part of traditional definition of inflation stealth inflation can also be calculated in service payment fees for example an electric bill can average from 119 99 or more when consumers go to pay the bill the processing fee can add an additional 5 6 to the subtotal some department stores have what is called the last 90 day policy a customer returning an item
