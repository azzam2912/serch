the store and museum are non profit and are run by the toy train depot foundation in january 2007 the alamogordo mcdonald s donated their ronald s railroad full sized standard gauge caboose to the museum the caboose is now in a high visibility location on us 54 70 alerting potential visitors to the museum s location guided tours by volunteer docents are available upon request the building that houses the museum began its life in 1898 as a working train depot in torrance new mexico at the junction of the new mexico central railroad and the el paso and northeastern railroad later the building was moved to corona new mexico where it remained for 75 years serving the rock island and southern pacific railroads the building retains its original stamped tin ceiling tiles as well its dispatcher s bay window
