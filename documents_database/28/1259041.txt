among his compositions were orchestral works including a symphony and a violin concerto and much chamber music including a string quartet born in new york city grasse was blind from infancy he began studying the piano at the age of 3 and the violin at age 5 he studied the violin in his native city with carl hauser for nine years before entering the royal conservatory of brussels in 1899 there he excelled under the instruction of c sar thomson earning a premier prix in violin and winning the prix de capacit in 1901 the latter prize had not been awarded in a decade in 1902 he made his debut as a concert violinist in berlin he spent the next few years performing throughout europe after returning to new york city he studied organ with daniel philippi he was active as a performer mainly in new york city through 1940 appearing regularly in concerts at carnegie hall steinway hall and town hall he died in new york city at the age of 69
