the church is recorded in the national heritage list for england as a designated grade i listed building it is an active anglican parish church in the diocese of chester the archdeaconry of chester and the deanery of great budworth it is one of three parish churches in the parish of aston by sutton little leigh and lower whitley the other two being st michael and all angels little leigh and st luke lower whitley the three were previously individual parishes united in a benefice along with st mark antrobus the listing describes it as a most pleasing late 17th to early 18th century church inside and out the church stands in a relatively isolated position in the south side of aston lane in the hamlet the first religious building in the hamlet was a chapel of ease in the parish of runcorn built in 1236 by sir thomas de dutton which was known as the chapel of poosey or pooseye towards the end of the 13th century the bishop of lichfield in whose diocese the chapel then was received a complaint and he gave orders that a chaplain and a lamp should be provided by the prior of norton
