courts commonly use orders to show cause when the judge needs more information before deciding whether or not to issue an order requested by one of the parties for example if a party requests that the court find another party in contempt of an existing court order the judge will typically issue an order to show cause re contempt to the party accused of being in contempt of court at the hearing on the order to show cause concerning contempt the judge will take evidence from both sides concerning the alleged failure to comply with the court order appellate courts often issue orders to show cause to lower courts requesting that the lower court explain why the appellant should not be granted the relief requested by the writ or appeal an order to show cause is always an interim order because it is never the first nor the final action in a legal action in some jurisdictions such as new york an order to show cause is used routinely to initiate a motion when a traditional notice of motion would not be sufficient for example when the moving party wishes to vary the usual schedule for considering a motion or
