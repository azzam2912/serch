morley was a case in english contract law that ran from 1596 to 1602 under the medieval common law claims seeking the repayment of a debt or other matters could only be pursued through a writ of debt in the court of common pleas a problematic and archaic process by 1558 the lawyers had succeeded in creating another method enforced by the court of king s bench through the action of assumpsit which was technically for deceit the legal fiction used was that by failing to pay after promising to do so a defendant had committed deceit and was liable to the plaintiff the conservative common pleas through the appellate court the court of exchequer chamber began to overrule decisions made by the king s bench on assumpsit causing friction between the courts in slade s case a case under assumpsit which was brought between judges of the common pleas and king s bench was transferred to the court of exchequer chamber where the king s bench judges were allowed to vote the case dragged on for five years with the judgment finally being delivered in 1602 by the chief justice of the king s bench john popham popham ruled
