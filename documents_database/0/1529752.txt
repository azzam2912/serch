the yellowish white conical shell is moderately depressed and slightly convex below it has a very strong rounded keel and is widely umbilicate the spire consists of 6 whorls the nucleus is smooth the upper whorls contain concentric ribs and traces of one or two spiral lirae producing small tubercles on the ribs the whorls are flattened near the suture above the upper row of tubercles this depression is roughened by small lamellae the lower part of the whorls is slightly convex on the lower whorls the sculpture consists of a row of rather strong tubercles at some distance from the suture the flattened space between suture and tubercles is also lamelliferous a second row of smaller tubercles makes its appearance and becomes more conspicuous on the body whorl at some distance from the lower suture a spiral rib with inconspicuous beads is crossed by irregular waved lamellae which spread on the large keel this keel is flat above rounded at its periphery and adorned by sharp compressed folds which make it crenulated the base of the shell is sculptured by five beaded spirals of which the outer one placed at some distance from the margin and the most central
