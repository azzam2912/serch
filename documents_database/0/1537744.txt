karkoschka was born in the german linguistic enclave of moravsk ostrava czechoslovakia and subsequent to world war ii became a violinist for the bayreuth symphony orchestra leading to studies in composition musicology and conducting at the musikhochschule in stuttgart and the university of t bingen germany his doctoral thesis was an analysis of the compositional techniques in the early works of anton webern from 1948 until 1968 he directed the choir and orchestra at the university of hohenheim the former agricultural college and the hohenheimer schlo konzerte in 1958 he taught at the state university of music and performing arts in stuttgart staatlichen hochschule f r musik und darstellende kunst stuttgart then in 1962 he founded his ensemble for new musik which eventually broke away from the school in 1976 and was renamed the contac ensemble in 1973 he became the director of the studio for electronic music in the stuttgart hochschule until his retirement in 1987 erhard karkoschka wrote a book on musical notation published in german english and japanese das schriftbild der neuen musik 1965 english trans notation in new music london new york 1972 japanese zen on music company ltd tokyo 1978 chinese translation 1999 the extent
