the committee meets twice yearly to review the latest research regarding vaccination against infectious diseases the stiko was established in 1972 as a department of the german federal health agency in berlin during a reorganization of the federal health agency in 1994 the stiko was attached to the robert koch institute rki which was now an independent upper level federal agency under the purview of the federal ministry of health bmg the legal basis for the stiko is 20 of the infection protection act or ifsg members of the expert body are appointed by the federal ministry of health in coordination with the state ministries of health additional expert representatives from the paul ehrlich institute robert koch institute federal and state ministries of health attend the meetings in an advisory capacity the commission s responsibility is to provide scientifically based recommendations regarding the necessary vaccinations in germany due to the importance of the recommendations these have been codified in the infection protection act since 2001 in accordance with the goals of the infection protection act the recommendations are focused on vaccinations that are most relevant to the protection of public health the law does not require the commission to perform
