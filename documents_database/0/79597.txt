the title was first adopted by robert de ferrers 1st earl of derby under a creation of 1139 it continued with the ferrers family until the 6th earl forfeited his property toward the end of the reign of henry iii and died in 1279 most of the ferrers property and by a creation in 1337 the derby title were then held by the family of henry iii the title merged in the crown upon henry iv s accession to the throne in 1399 the title was created again this time for the stanley family in 1485 lord derby s subsidiary titles are baron stanley of bickerstaffe in the county palatine of lancaster created 1832 and baron stanley of preston in the county palatine of lancaster 1886 the courtesy title of the heir apparent is lord stanley the 1st to 5th earls also held an earlier barony of stanley created for the 1st earl s father in 1456 and currently abeyant the 2nd to 5th earls held the barony of strange created in 1299 currently held by the viscounts st davids and the 7th to 9th earls held another barony of strange created in error in 1628 and currently held independently
