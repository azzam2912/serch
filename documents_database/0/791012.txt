the head and back are covered in dark brown fur that varies from reddish to almost black while the sides and underparts are a paler grey colour the ears are large and rounded with a small pointed tragus and dark hairless skin the wing membranes are also dark and have narrow tips the fur extends across the upper surface of the wing up to a line between the knees and the midpoint of the humerus the face is almost entirely hairless and ends in a blunt muzzle adult males possess a sacular organ on the throat the function of which is unknown but which is only vestigial in females the bat most closely resembles the related wagner s bonneted bat but the latter usually has a paler colour and has a different shape to the tragus the black bonneted bat is found in southern mexico on the island of jamaica and through much of central and south america as far south as northern argentina it is however absent from western peru and bolivia and from chile it inhabits a range of forested habitats throughout this region from coastal lowlands to the slopes of the andes at least in elevation in
