one of his centurions was put in charge of a group of captives including chiomara described as a woman of exceptional beauty he made sexual advances towards her and when these were rejected raped her he then offered to assuage his shame to ransom her back to her people sending one of her slaves also a captive with the message her countrymen came to the appointed place with the ransom but while the centurion was counting the gold chiomara indicated to them with a nod according to plutarch or by speaking to them in their own language according to livy and valerius maximus that they were to cut off his head she then carried the head home wrapped in the folds of her dress and threw it at the feet of her husband telling him that only one man who has been intimate with her shall remain alive the greek historian polybius is said to have met her at sardis and been impressed with her good sense and intelligence
