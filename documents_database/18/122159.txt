chungcheong was located in the southwest of korea the provincial capital was located at gongju which had been the capital of the kingdom of baekje from 475 to 538 chungcheong province was formed in 1356 during the goryeo dynasty from the southern portion of the former province of yanggwang its name derived from the names of the principal cities of chungju and cheongju in 1895 the province was replaced by the districts of chungju chungju bu in the east gongju gongju bu in the centre and hongju hongju bu modern day hongseong county in the west in 1896 chungju and eastern gongju districts were reorganized into north chungcheong province and hongju and western gongju districts were reorganized into south chungcheong province north and south chungcheong are today part of south korea historic chungcheong was bordered on the north by gyeonggi province on the north east by gangwon the east by gyeongsang provinces on the south by north jeolla province and on the west by the yellow sea the region is mountainous in the east the north province and somewhat lower and flatter in the west the south province the regional name for chungcheong is hoseo although this name is used
