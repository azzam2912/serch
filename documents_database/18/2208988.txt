it was listed on the national register of historic places in 1976 the historic designation includes a large house c 1860 a smaller house c 1855 and a barn all constructed in locally quarried limestone there are very few agricultural stone buildings in iowa the main house is the most sophisticated of the three the two story structure features dressed limestone blocks laid in a random ashlar pattern a front gable main block and a single story dining kitchen wing the older house is a single story structure composed of rubble stone the barn s western most section has a unique cube shape the o hair family acquired the property in the early 1850s and built the smaller house and the first two sections of the barn elizabeth strawn two times a widow moved her family to the farm around 1860 she had the large house and the third section of the barn constructed she moved into charles city around 1865 and sold the farm to a w cook who remained here until his death in 1887 he is believed to be the first person in iowa to raise percheron s which he imported from france he also raised shorthorn
