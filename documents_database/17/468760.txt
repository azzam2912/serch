on a person s distal forearm just before the wrist there are either two or three tendons the tendon of the flexor carpi ulnaris is the most medial closest to the little finger of these the most lateral one is the tendon of flexor carpi radialis muscle and the middle one not always present is the tendon of palmaris longus the muscle like all flexors of the forearm can be strengthened by exercises that resist its flexion a wrist roller can be used and wrist curls with dumbbells can also be performed these exercises are used to prevent injury to the ulnar collateral ligament of elbow joint ulnar entrapment by the aponeurosis of the two heads of the flexor carpi ulnaris muscle may cause cubital tunnel syndrome
