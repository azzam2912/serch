the square was named after california architect albert starr spaulding who developed the eight block area between 1916 and 1926 most of the early residents were silent film stars and directors one early resident was rudolph j berquist a renowned cinematographer known for early works like camille 1921 film and one night in rome he built his personal residence at 1400 n ogden drive in 1919 and the house is now part of the hpoz remaining in perfect condition to this day in 1993 spaulding square was designated a historic preservation overlay zone hpoz by the city of los angeles with the object to preserve the historic architecture found in the community
