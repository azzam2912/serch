the 13th named storm of the 1995 96 australian region cyclone season olivia formed on 3 april 1996 to the north of australia s northern territory the storm moved generally to the southwest gradually intensifying off western australia on 8 april olivia intensified into a severe tropical cyclone and subsequently turned more to the south steered by a passing trough on 10 april olivia produced the worldwide record strongest gust on barrow island and on the same day the cyclone made landfall near varanus island the storm quickly weakened over land dissipating over the great australian bight on 12 april while in its formative stages olivia produced light rainfall in the northern territory while offshore western australia the cyclone forced oil platforms to shut down and the combination of high winds and waves caused heavy damage to oil facilities onshore olivia s high winds damaged several small mining towns halting operations every house in pannawonica sustained some damage one person in the town was injured by flying glass and had to be flown to receive treatment and nine others were lightly injured the cyclone also produced heavy rainfall and a localized storm surge damage was estimated in the millions while
