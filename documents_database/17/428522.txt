in gascony according to the families perpetuating the tradition of duck confit all the pieces of duck are used to produce the meal each part can have a specific destination in traditional cooking the neck being used for example in an invigorating soup the garbure duck confit is considered one of the finest french dishes while it is made across france it is seen as a specialty of gascony the confit is prepared in a centuries old process of preservation that consists of salt curing a piece of meat generally goose duck or pork and then cooking it in its own fat to prepare a confit the meat is rubbed with salt garlic and sometimes herbs such as thyme then covered and refrigerated for up to 36 hours salt curing the meat acts as a preservative prior to cooking the spices are rinsed from the meat which is then patted dry the meat is placed in a cooking dish deep enough to contain the meat and the rendered fat and placed in an oven at a low temperature 76 135 degrees celsius 170 275 fahrenheit the meat is slowly poached at least until cooked or until meltingly tender generally four
