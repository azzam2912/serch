as a young man he purchased his own freedom for 800 he traveled to indiana and soon moved to milwaukee wisconsin where he sold groceries and then worked as a railroad porter he quickly became a leader in the african american community he operated a local branch of the underground railroad pushed richard allen bishop to open wisconsin first african american church and also played a role in the joshua glover controversy in 1865 at the insistence of sherman booth gillespie attempted to vote he was denied a ballot so he sued the board of elections gillespie v palmer went all the way to the wisconsin supreme court the justices of the court sided with gillespie in his argument that wisconsin voters had voted in favor of male african american suffrage in an 1849 referendum gillespie later moved to chicago illinois where he died on march 31 1892 his remains were brought back to milwaukee to be buried in forest home cemetery alongside some of milwaukee s most famous residents
