many of the baltic psalteries hold a strong symbolic significance in their respective countries including finland latvia lithuania estonia and russia where playing instruction and instrument makers are available according to finnish linguist eino nieminen the names kankl s kantele kannel k ndla and kokles possibly come from the proto baltic form kantl s kantl s which originally meant the singing tree ultimately deriving from the proto european root qan to sing to sound however lithuanian ethnologist romualdas apanavi ius believes they could be derived from the proto european root gan dh meaning a vessel a haft of a sword suggesting that it may be related to the russian word gusli since 1990 baltic psaltery symposia have taken place in finland 1990 1997 2008 lithuania 1994 2017 latvia 2000 canada 2004 and estonia 2013 every three or four years on a rotating basis
