representative from texas great uncle of robert c eckhardt and uncle of richard m kleberg sr born in cat spring texas kleberg was instructed by private tutors and graduated from concrete college de witt county in 1868 he enlisted in tom green s brigade of cavalry in the confederate states army in the spring of 1864 and served until the close of the civil war after the war kleberg studied law in san antonio he was admitted to the bar in 1872 kleberg commenced practice in cuero texas the next year he founded the cuero star in 1873 after a few years of practice kleberg was appointed as prosecuting attorney of de witt county 1876 1890 entering electoral politics he served as a member of the state senate 1882 1886 in 1885 he was appointed as united states attorney for the western district of texas kleberg was elected as a democrat to the fifty fourth congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of william h crain he was reelected to the fifty fifth fifty sixth and fifty seventh congresses and served from april 7 1896 to march 3 1903 he was not a candidate for renomination in 1902
