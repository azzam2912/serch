prior to becoming a nature reserve it was used as farmland and for sand quarrying it is managed by wardens employed by fcc environment operators of nearby arpley landfill site arpley landfill began operating in 1988 and covers more than 500 acres the landfill no longer accepts waste and is being restored to a mix of woodland and grassland and together with moore nature reserve will eventually provide an important public amenity and area of rich biodiversity on the edge of what is a busy and growing urban area access is made freely available to the public and there are maintained footpaths around the reserve there are five lakes woodland meadows bird feeding stations and nine bird hides covering the lakes the reserve is one of the best in cheshire and hosts all three species of woodpecker including the lesser spotted woodpeckers and five species of british owls including tawny owls
