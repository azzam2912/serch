he spent most of his adult life in the civil and military service of the colony and commanded a sloop for chasing privateers wanton was the third son of edward wanton a ship builder after witnessing their persecution he became a quaker and preacher his father had lived in york maine boston massachusetts and scituate massachusetts before coming to rhode island wanton was a merchant who became a freeman of newport rhode island in 1698 and was thereafter very active in the civil and military affairs of the town and colony in all but two years from 1705 to 1732 wanton was either a deputy or an assistant and for many of those years he was the speaker of the house of deputies in the years 1705 to 1706 and 1707 to 1710 he was the major for the islands having command of the militia companies on aquidneck connanicut prudence and block islands from 1719 to 1731 he was in command as colonel of the militia of the islands regiment wanton commanded a sloop that he used to chase privateers and in 1709 the general assembly voted to buy his new sloop diamond for 400 pounds and also buy another
