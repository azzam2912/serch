a native of oregon he wrote for the new york times before and after a stint in the u s army during world war ii a democrat he entered politics in his home state by winning a seat in the oregon house of representatives and later was elected to the united states senate his widow maurine brown neuberger won his senate seat after his death neuberger was born on december 26 1912 in the rural part of multnomah county oregon the son of ruth lewis and isaac neuberger restaurant owners his grandparents were all german jewish immigrants neuberger grew up in nearby portland he graduated from the university of oregon in 1935 and served as editor of the student newspaper the oregon daily emerald neuberger began writing for the new york times as a college senior and became the newspaper s northwest correspondent in 1939 he also began writing books during these years in 1940 neuberger was elected to the oregon house of representatives his political career was interrupted by world war ii during which neuberger served in the u s army as an officer from 1942 to 1945 back in civilian life neuberger continued to work for the times
