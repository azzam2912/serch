it was long thought to be the actual burial place of cwichelm but the mound has been excavated several times without serious finds in 1006 the anglo saxon chronicle states that the danes marched to cuckhamsley hill as they believed that if they reached the hill they would never return to the sea the knob appears to have once been a round barrow but now is a semi circular bank with a round front its appearance may have changed in part due to vandalism based on the belief it was used to hoard treasure the name scutchamer may have derived from cwichelmeshlaew an alternate explanation is that the name is based on the word scutcher someone who scutched or beat out flax to loosen its fibres and the knob was a location of local fairs trading in local products deriving from this practice
