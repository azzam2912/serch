while studying pharmacy at purdue university whittall won two gold medals at the 1955 pan american games in mexico city she also won the 100 metre butterfly and the 400 metre freestyle events and was a member of the canadian 4x400 metre medley relay team that won a silver medal for those achievements she was awarded the lou marsh trophy as canada s top athlete for 1955 and was inducted into the canadian olympic hall of fame at the end of that year she held five canadian swimming records including the 110 yard butterfly and the one mile swim she finished seventh in the 100 metre butterfly at the 1956 summer olympics and retired from competition the following year in 1987 at the age of 50 whittall set a canadian record in the 200 metre freestyle for competitors in the 50 to 54 age group whittall died on may 1 2015 at the age of 78 and was posthumously inducted into the canadian olympic hall of fame on june 17 2015
