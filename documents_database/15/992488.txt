the museum was created to serve as an institution to research preserve and interpret the treasures of angling history today the museum serves as a repository for and conservator to the world s largest collection of angling and angling related items numbering in the thousands the collections and exhibits document the evolution of fly fishing as a sport art form craft and industry in the united states and abroad dating as far back as the sixteenth century rods reels flies tackle art photographs manuscripts and books form the museum s permanent collection including the oldest documented flies in the world the museum has been accredited by the american alliance of museums since 1993 and received reaccreditation in 2009 the museum is one of approximately 776 museums across the nation with this designation and one of only five in the state of vermont since 1974 the american museum of fly fishing has published the journal the american fly fisher
