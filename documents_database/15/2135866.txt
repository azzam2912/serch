he had spent one year at amherst college before entering as sophomore at yale college where he graduated in 1836 he taught a high school in canaan conn until the spring of 1837 when he began law studies with judge arphaxad loomis of little falls new york upon his admission to the bar in 1839 he decided to emigrate to the west he selected st louis and arrived there in november 1839 beginning practice in march 1840 he gave special attention to questions affecting real property and achieved distinguished success in litigated cases of this nature through apprehensions with regard to his health he retired in 1860 at a comparatively early age from active practice confining himself thereafter to office consultations he declined all political nominations until 1854 when he was elected to the missouri house of representatives in 1860 he was an unsuccessful candidate for us congress on the bell and everett ticket in 1875 he was a member of the convention which revised the constitution of missouri he was a director of washington university in st louis and served gratuitously for fifteen years in its law department as lecturer among his students was lemma barkaloo the first female
