in 1897 he traveled for the first time to europe studying in rome with the spaniards salvador s nchez barbudo and mariano barbas n he continued his studies in spain with joaqu n sorolla y bastida in 1902 he took his second and final trip to europe as a specialist in portraits herrera was popular among the high society of montevideo his portraits of women and children display his mastery of pastel his favored medium he also delved like many of his contemporaries into the genre of nativismo painting scenes of gauchos and criollos he also painted in the historical genre his most popular works in this genre being congreso de abril de 1813 and artigas en el hervidero which appears on the reverse of the 1000 note some of his portraits are in permanent display in the national museum of visual arts in montevideo
