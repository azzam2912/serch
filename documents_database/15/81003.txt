the esrb was established in 1994 by the entertainment software association formerly the interactive digital software association in response to criticism of controversial video games with excessively violent or sexual content the board assigns ratings to games based on their content using judgment similar to the motion picture rating systems used in many countries using a combination of six age based levels intended to aid consumers in determining a game s content and suitability along with a system of content descriptors which detail specific types of content present in a particular game the esrb maintains a code of ethics for the advertising and promotion of video games ensuring that marketing materials for games are targeted to appropriate audiences in 2011 the esrb began offering a system to automatically assign ratings for digitally distributed games and mobile apps which utilizes a survey answered by the product s publisher as opposed to a manual assessment by esrb staff through the international age rating coalition iarc this method can generate equivalent ratings for other territories alongside its game rating operation the esrb also provides certification services for online privacy on websites and mobile apps the esrb ratings system is enforced via the voluntary
