it includes the hamlet of jurij ob pesnici on the left bank of the pesnica river as well as the dispersed houses in the hills north of the village the name of the settlement was changed from sveti jurij ob pesnici literally saint george on the pesnica river to jurski vrh literally george peak in 1952 the name was changed on the basis of the 1948 law on names of settlements and designations of squares streets and buildings as part of efforts by slovenia s postwar communist government to remove religious elements from toponyms the parish church from which the village gets its name is dedicated to saint george and belongs to the roman catholic archdiocese of maribor it stands in the centre of the village on the north side of the main road the church was first mentioned in written documents dating to 1383 the current building dates to the 16th century and is a typical example of gothic architecture of the region with a single nave it was renovated in the 17th century and in 1855 and contains baroque internal furnishings the date 1532 appears on the belfry
