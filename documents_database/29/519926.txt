alpha sagittae is the bayer designation which is latinized from sagittae and abbreviated alpha sge or sge it is visible to the naked eye as a yellow hued star with an apparent visual magnitude of 4 39 despite the name this is not the brightest star in the constellation that distinction belongs to gamma sagittae based upon parallax measurements alpha sagittae is approximately 382 light years from the sun it is moving further away from the earth with a heliocentric radial velocity of 1 7 km s this is an evolved bright giant with a stellar classification of g1 ii it is 151 million years old with 4 times the mass of the sun and has expanded to around 21 times the sun s radius it is radiating 340 times the sun s luminosity from its enlarged photosphere at an effective temperature of 5 333 k there is an x ray source within of these coordinates this star bore the traditional name sham or alsahm which derives from the arabic word sahm meaning arrow the name formerly having been applied to the whole constellation in 2016 the international astronomical union organized a working group on star names wgsn to catalogue
