opened in 1933 it is the home field of the california golden bears of the pac 12 with a seating capacity of 2 500 evans diamond is located in the uc sports complex in the southwest corner of campus pressed between george c edwards stadium to the west right field and haas pavilion to the east originally named edwards field it was renamed after clint evans the cal head coach from 1930 54 the stadium was renovated in 1992 at a cost of 275 000 paid for by the donations of uc alumni construction was done by rnt landscaping a san leandro landscaping company the turf at evans diamond is natural grass and the infield dirt is a combination of crushed cinder and the traditional clay the outfield wall is 320 feet 98 m from home plate in the corners 365 feet 111 m in the power alleys and 395 feet 120 m in center field in the right field corner are the batting cages and pitching machines covered by a roof but not enclosed the bullpen is outside of the roof closer to the warning track in the left field corner is the other bullpen without batting cages both
