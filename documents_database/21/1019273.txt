it was made up almost exclusively of german hungarian czech and slovak immigrants it was the first unit mobilised for the war in chicago and was composed of many forty eighters veterans of the revolutions of 1848 in germany and the austrian empire the 24th illinois infantry was organized at chicago illinois and recruited from counties of cook mclean and lasalle and mustered into federal service on july 8 1861 the regiment was divided into companies from a to k it was assigned to the army of the ohio in november 1861 and later was transformed to the army of the cumberland in november 1862 the regiment was mustered out on august 6 1864 the regiment suffered 3 officers and 86 enlisted men who were killed in action or who died of their wounds and 2 officers and 82 enlisted men who died of disease for a total of 173 fatalities
