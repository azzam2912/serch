it is a tree with papery bark narrow leaves and spikes of white or creamy coloured flowers usually growing along streams and rivers it is common in tropical areas and is sometimes confused with melaleuca argentea although it lacks that species silvery foliage melaleuca fluviatilis is a tree growing up to tall with white or greyish papery bark and weeping habit its leaves are arranged alternately long and wide very narrow elliptical in shape and with 5 to 7 parallel veins both surfaces of the leaf are covered with fine soft hairs when young but become glabrous as they mature the flowers are white to creamy green and arranged in spikes in the upper leaf axils each spike contains 3 to 18 groups of flowers in threes and is up to long and in diameter the petals are long and fall off after the flower opens the stamens are arranged in five bundles around the flower each bundle containing 3 to 9 stamens flowers appear from may to october and are followed by fruit which are woody capsules long melaleuca fluviatilis was first formally described in 1997 by bryan barlow in nomen from a specimen found in a sandy river
