it was a major comeback hit released in 1969 as a 45 rpm single with any day now as the flip side it is a narrative of generational poverty a boy is born to a mother who already has more children than she can feed in the ghetto of chicago the boy grows up hungry steals and fights purchases a gun and steals a car attempts to run but is shot and killed the song ends with another child being born in the ghetto and implies that the newborn could meet the same fate continuing the cycle of poverty and violence the feeling of an inescapable circle is created by the structure of the song with its simple stark phrasing by the repetition of the phrase in the ghetto as the close of every fourth line and finally by the repetition of the first verse s and his mama cries just before the beginning and as the close of the last verse it is played in the key of b flat in the ghetto was recorded during presley s session in the american sound studio in memphis tennessee it was presley s first creative recording session since the 68 comeback
