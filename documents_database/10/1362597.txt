if only one bar appears across the middle of the shield it is termed a fess if two or more appear they can only be called bars calling the bar a diminutive of the fess is inaccurate however because two bars may each be no smaller than a fess like the fess bars too may bear complex lines such as embattled indented nebuly etc the diminutive form of the bar narrower than a bar yet wider than a cottise is the barrulet though these frequently appear in pairs the pair termed a bar gemel rather than two barrulets a single bar placed across the top of the field is called a chief a single bar placed over the center of the field is called a fess two to four of these appearing on a shield are called bars and more than four are called barrulets thin bars are termed barrulets a still thinner bar or riband is known as a cottise cottises never appear alone and have no direction of their own but are borne on each side of an ordinary such as a fess pale bend or chevron the ordinary thus accompanied by a cottise on each side is
