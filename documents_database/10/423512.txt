it is a traditional dish in afghanistan albania armenia azerbaijan bosnia and herzegovina bulgaria georgia greece iran iraq mongolia and turkey the name khash originates from the armenian verb which means to boil the dish initially called khashoy is mentioned by a number of medieval armenian authors including grigor magistros 11th century mkhitar heratsi 12th century and yesayi nchetsi 13th century the persian term literally means trotter the combination of a sheep s head and trotters is called which literally means head and trotter in persian khash is a purist meal with great parsimony in ingredients the feet are depilated cleaned kept in cold water in order to get rid of bad smell and boiled in water all night long until the water has become a thick broth and the meat has separated from the bones no salt or spices are added during the boiling process the dish is served hot one may add salt garlic lemon juice or vinegar according to one s taste dried lavash is often crumbled into the broth to add substance khash is generally served with a variety of other foods such as hot green and yellow peppers pickles radishes cheese and fresh greens such
