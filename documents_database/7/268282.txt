things are deemed to have instrumental value if they help one achieve a particular end intrinsic values in contrast are understood to be desirable in and of themselves a tool or appliance such as a hammer or washing machine have instrumental value because one helps you pound in a nail and the other makes it possible to clean your clothes happiness and pleasure are typically considered to have intrinsic value insofar as asking why someone would want them makes little sense they are desirable for their own sake irrespective of their possible utility the classic names instrumental and intrinsic were coined by sociologist max weber who spent years studying good meanings people assigned to their actions and beliefs here are weber s original definitions with a comment showing his doubt that conditionally efficient means can achieve unconditionally legitimate ends followed by three modern definitions from the oxford handbook of value theory when people judge efficient means and legitimate ends at the same time both can be considered as good but when ends are judged separately from means it may result in a conflict what works may not be right what is right may not work separating the criteria contaminates reasoning
