her father was a marble dealer she was raised in thomasville georgia she studied piano performance at brenau college graduating in 1905 cocroft was a church organist and choir director in thomasville her husband ran a music store and published her compositions to sell in the shop under the name n weldon cocroft after her divorce she moved to jacksonville florida where she worked in a law office published works by nellie weldon cocroft included kute kids 1909 the pinywoods rag 1909 when the autumn turns the forest leaves to gold 1909 the georgia cracker march and two step 1910 and i se gwine to highball 1910 nellie estelle weldon married fellow musician christopher columbus cocroft in 1905 in thomasville their son charles christopher cocroft 1910 2001 continued the family s music publishing company until he sold the business in 2000 the cocrofts divorced in 1924 she died in 1986 aged 100 years music by nellie weldon cocroft is part of the permanent jazz swing club exhibit at the georgia music hall of fame in macon pianist nora hulse included cocroft s georgia cracker on her ragtime refreshments 2002 a collection of works by women
