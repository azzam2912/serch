municipally bamut is incorporated as bamutskoye rural settlement it is the administrative center of the municipality and is the only settlement included in it bamut is located on both banks of the fortanga river it is located west of the town of achkhoy martan and west of the city of grozny the nearest settlements to bamut are katyr yurt in the east shalazhi stary achkhoy and yandi in the south east arshty in the south west nesterovskaya in the north west and assinovskaya and novy sharoy in the north from 1922 to 1934 bamut was a part of the ingush autonomous oblast in 1944 after the genocide and deportation of the chechen and ingush people and the chechen ingush assr was abolished the village of bamut was renamed to bukovka and settled by people from the neighboring republic of dagestan from 1944 to 1957 it was a part of the vedensky district of the dagestan assr in 1958 after the vaynakh people returned and the chechen ingush assr was restored the village regained its old chechen name bummat during the first chechen war the infamous battle of bamut occurred in the village at the start of the first chechen war
