some australian state herbaria continue to use the name callistemon hemistictus it is a tall bushy shrub with dark green leaves and red flowers spikes tipped with yellow melaleuca hemisticta is a shrub growing to tall with grey papery or fibrous bark its leaves are arranged alternately and are long wide flat mostly narrow egg shaped with a mid vein and 15 to 30 branching veins the flowers are arranged in spikes on the ends of branches which continue to grow after flowering and sometimes on the sides of the branches the spikes are in diameter with 10 to 50 individual flowers the petals are long and fall off as the flower ages there are 35 53 stamens in each flower with red filaments and yellow anthers flowering occurs from march to september and is followed by fruit which are woody capsules long melaleuca hemisticta was first formally described in 2006 by lyndley craven in novon the specific epithet hemisticta is from the greek prefix hemi meaning half and stiktos meaning spotted referring to the oil glands in the leaves being only in the lower half of the leaf callistemon hemistictus is regarded as a synonym of melaleuca hemisticta by
