6th street in cleveland ohio in the united states it was the first steel frame skyscraper constructed in the city the edifice was designed by henry ives cobb and built in 1893 by harry augustus garfield and james rudolph garfield sons of president james a garfield the ten story structure was the first steel frame office building erected in the city the facade on e 6th street was long taking up the entire block between euclid and vincent avenues but just on euclid avenue patrons could access the upper floors via a large marble staircase or four elevators all the upper floors featured italian marble wainscoting a below ground level was intended to serve as a bank and several meeting rooms banking parlors and massive steel vaults were erected there during the building s construction at a cost of 100 000 in dollars a customer for this space was not found until 1895 when the cleveland trust company moved in the building s major tenant was the local luxury jewelry firm of cowell and hubbard which rented the entire first floor in 1898 the cleveland trust co moved a portion of its operations onto the first floor building club rooms
