he began his career in 1947 as a teacher at the allgemeine gewerbeschule basel school of art and crafts at the age of twenty six hofmann followed emil ruder as head of the graphic design department at the schule f r gestaltung basel basel school of design and was instrumental in developing the graphic design style known as the swiss style his teaching methods were unorthodox and broad based setting new standards that became widely known in design education institutions throughout the world his independent insights as an educator married with his rich and innovative powers of visual expression created a body of work enormously varied books exhibitions stage sets logotypes symbols typography posters sign systems and environmental graphics his work is recognized for its reliance on the fundamental elements of graphic form point line and shape while subtly conveying simplicity complexity representation and abstraction originating in russia germany and the netherlands in the 1920s stimulated by the artistic avant garde and alongside the international style in architecture he is well known for his posters which emphasized economical use of colour and fonts in reaction to what hofmann regarded as the trivialization of colour his posters have been widely exhibited
