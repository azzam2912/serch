typically medical advice involves giving a diagnosis and or prescribing a treatment for medical condition medical advice can be distinguished from medical information which is the relation of facts discussing facts and information is considered a fundamental free speech right and is not considered medical advice medical advice can also be distinguished from personal advice even if the advice concerns medical care medical advice is given in the context of a doctor patient relationship a licensed health care professional can be held legally liable for the advice he or she gives to a patient giving bad advice may be considered medical malpractice under specified circumstances the doctor patient relationship is one factor in determining the patient s compliance with medical advice patients adhere more closely to medical advice when the healthcare provider is friendly doesn t interrupt the patient or has good verbal communication skills patients are less likely to comply with medical advice if the advice is not what the patients expected if the patients do not agree with the proposed treatment if the patient is not confident in the provider s competence or if the patient cannot understand what the provider says due to language barriers or overuse
