the apostolic constitution spirituali militum curae of 21 april 1986 raised their status declaring that the bishop who heads one of them is an ordinary holding authority by virtue of his office and not by delegation from another person in authority it likened the military vicariates to dioceses each of them is headed by a bishop who may have the personal rank of archbishop if the bishop is a diocesan rather than a titular bishop he is likely to delegate the daily functions to an auxiliary bishop or a lower cleric some nations have military ordinariates of the anglican communion lutheranism and eastern orthodoxy the personal ordinariates for anglicans entering the catholic church announced on 20 october 2009 are similar in some ways to the existing military ordinariates but the jurisdiction of military ordinariates is cumulative to that of the diocesan bishops
