the novel is based on the life of bushranger jimmy governor the subject of an earlier book by frank clune the story is written from the perspective of jimmy blacksmith an indigenous australian man on a mission of revenge the story is a fictionalised retelling of the life of the infamous indigenous bushranger jimmy governor keneally has said were he to write the novel in the present day he would not presume to write in the voice of an indigenous australian the novel was nominated for the booker prize in 1972 losing to john berger s g the film won the best original music score bruce smeaton best actress in a lead role angela punch mcgregor and best actor in a supporting role ray barrett and was nominated for 9 more awards at the australian film institute afi for 1978 it was also nominated for the palme d or golden palm at the 1978 cannes film festival the chant of jimmie blacksmith by australian roots rock band the groovesmiths is also based on the story
