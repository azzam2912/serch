the diocese is centred in the city of po ega it was first erected in 1997 after being split from the archdiocese of zagreb the archdiocese had grown too large to effectively serve its faithful po ega was selected as the seat of diocese because of its historic tradition as an urban centre since the 13th century it was also the site of the first institution of higher education in slavonia the jesuit academy established in 1761 the diocese covers the territory of western slavonia as well as parts of podravina virovitica and posavina nova gradi ka this region suffered greatly during the croatian war of independence and the need for the church to help in the recovery was another reason for the diocese s creation many church holdings were destroyed during the course of the war and the recovery is still an ongoing process the diocese has 270 000 faithful who are organized into the deaneries of na ice nova gradi ka nova kapela pakrac po ega and virovitica with a total of 85 parishes currently bishop antun kvor evi is head of the diocese the diocesan cathedral is saint teresa of vila cathedral on june 18 2008 the
