the foliage is strongly aromatic with a spicy resinous scent the leaves are of two forms juvenile needle like leaves 8 10 mm long on seedlings and irregularly on adult plants and adult scale leaves 0 6 3 mm long on older plants they are arranged in decussate opposite pairs it is dioecious with separate male and female plants the cones are berry like 7 12 mm in diameter blue black with a whitish waxy bloom and contain 1 4 seeds they are mature in about 18 months the male cones are 3 4 mm long and shed their pollen in early spring there are two varieties regarded as distinct by some authors but not by others overall the species is not considered threatened with healthy population in spain however the african population is threatened by severe overgrazing mainly by goats and is listed as endangered in the sierra de solorio there is the largest spanish juniper forest in europe
