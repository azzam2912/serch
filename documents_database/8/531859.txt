as a member of the irish parliamentary party he was member of parliament mp for east tyrone from 1906 to 1910 at westminster he joined the irish volunteers in 1913 then on the outbreak of world war i in 1914 enlisted for service in the british army with which he was killed in action on the western front in the autumn of 1916 he was a much admired old comrade of james joyce who considered him to be his best friend in ireland as well as the likes of francis sheehy skeffington oliver st john gogarty and robert wilson lynd he was one of the leading figures of the generation who at the turn of the twentieth century gave new intellectual life to irish party politics and to the constitutional movement towards all ireland home rule a gifted speaker with an incisive mind and devastating wit his death was regarded as a great loss to ireland s political and intellectual life as g k chesterton surmised thomas michael kettle was perhaps the greatest example of that greatness of spirit which was so ill rewarded on both sides of the channel he was a wit a scholar an orator a man
