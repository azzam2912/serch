negotiations between the three great powers britain france and russia resulted in the establishment of the kingdom of greece under a bavarian prince the decisions were ratified in the treaty of constantinople later that year the treaty followed the akkerman convention which had previously recognized another territorial change in the balkans the suzerainty of principality of serbia greece had won its independence from the ottoman empire in the greek war of independence 1821 1829 with the help of britain france and russia in the london protocol of 3 february 1830 the three powers had assigned the borders of the new state however when the governor of greece john capodistria ioannis kapodistrias was assassinated in 1831 in nafplion the greek peninsula plunged into confusion the great powers sought a formal end of the war and a recognized government in greece in may 1832 british foreign secretary palmerston convened with french and russian diplomats and without consultation of the greeks decided that greece should be a monarchy the convention offered the throne to the bavarian prince otto they also established the line of succession which would pass the crown to otto s descendants or his younger brothers should he have no issue
