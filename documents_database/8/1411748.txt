researchers in the field of hci observe the ways in which humans interact with computers and design technologies that let humans interact with computers in novel ways as a field of research human computer interaction is situated at the intersection of computer science behavioural sciences design media studies and several other fields of study the term was popularized by stuart k card allen newell and thomas p moran in their seminal 1983 book the psychology of human computer interaction although the authors first used the term in 1980 and the first known use was in 1975 the term connotes that unlike other tools with only limited uses such as a hammer useful for driving nails but not much else a computer has many uses and this takes place as an open ended dialog between the user and the computer the notion of dialog likens human computer interaction to human to human interaction an analogy which is crucial to theoretical considerations in the field humans interact with computers in many ways the interface between humans and computers is crucial to facilitate this interaction desktop applications internet browsers handheld computers and computer kiosks make use of the prevalent graphical user interfaces gui
