the cricket writer a g moyes said of hack s career that he did grand service for south australia as an opening batsman standing a few inches over six feet he was an extremely difficult man to dislodge once he dug in his heels never quite test class he was nevertheless a remarkably handy man for the ordinary first class game he continued playing senior cricket in sydney into the 1920s for some years hack was the managing director of his own company f t hack limited which manufactured automobiles in adelaide after he left adelaide to take up business in sydney in 1917 the company s premises were bought by holden in 1924 he was manager of the missenden road body building works in the sydney suburb of camperdown he and his wife had two daughters and two sons both of whom played cricket for south australia
