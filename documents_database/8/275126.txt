a pasar malam is a street market in indonesia malaysia brunei and singapore that opens in the evening usually in residential neighbourhoods it brings together a collection of stalls that usually sell goods such as snacks local favourite dishes fruits clothes shoes toys balloons watch and alarm clocks knick knacks and ornaments at cheap or at least reasonable prices unauthorized copies of dvds cds and computer software are often sold at a pasar malam pasar malam might resemble a night festival or a fairground where fair games and kiddy rides like mini carousel or mini train ride may also present several quintessential fair snacks like cotton candy ice cream hot dogs and grilled sausages are also popular next to the offerings of traditional local delicacies a pasar malam often takes place only one to a few days of the week as the traders rotate around different neighbourhoods on different days of the week haggling over prices is a common practice at such markets in indonesian archipelago markets are traditionally held on different days with locations rotating among participating villages this traditional economic custom is known as hari pasaran lit market days in javanese after being further developed market was established
