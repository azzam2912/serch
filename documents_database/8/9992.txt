whereas plumcots and apriplums are first generation hybrids between a plum parent p salicina or p cerasifera or their hybrids and an apricot p armeniaca pluots and apriums are later generations both names plumcot and apriplum have been used for trees derived from a plum seed parent and are therefore equivalent natural plumcots also called apriplums have been known for hundreds of years from regions of the world that grow both plums and apricots from seed the name plumcot was created by luther burbank the plumcot tree can reproduce asexually by budding whereas the apriplum tree resulted from hybridized seedlings and cannot reproduce pluots are later generations of complex hybrid between the japanese plum prunus salicina providing the greater amount of parentage and the apricot prunus armeniaca the fruit s exterior has smooth skin closely resembling that of a plum pluots were developed in the late 20th century by floyd zaiger apriums are complex plum apricot hybrids that show primarily apricot traits and flavor apriums resemble apricots on the outside the flesh is usually dense and notable for its sweet taste due to a high content of fructose and other sugars apriums are usually only available early in the fruit
