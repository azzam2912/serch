created in response to streaming platforms like netflix and roku in the west the name comes from the korean word manbang meaning everywhere or every direction conveying the on demand nature of the service due to north korean s isolationism users connect to the service not by internet but via the state controlled intranet using the iptv protocol in addition to on demand video manbang is reported to offer viewers the ability to watch live streams of at least 5 channels users may also find political information regarding the supreme leader and juche ideology and read articles from the newspaper rodong sinmun and the korean central news agency kcna worker education services for north korean enterprises are also available via the manbang service according to korean central television kctv viewers can use the service not only in pyongyang but also in sariwon and sinuiju a region in which kctv states demand for the equipment is particularly high with several hundred users in the region
