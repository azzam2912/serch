there are several areas with skeletal remains in the main chamber the best known is the crystal maiden the skeleton of an adolescent an 18 year old girl possibly a sacrifice victim whose bones have been calcified to a sparkling crystallized appearance the ceramics at the site are significant partly because they are marked with kill holes which indicate that they were used for ceremonial purposes many of the maya artifacts and remains are completely calcified to the cave floor one artifact named the monkey pot is one of just four of its type found in central america the maya also modified cave formations here in some instances to create altars for the offerings in others to create silhouettes of faces and animals or to project a shadow image into the cave the cave is extensively decorated with cave formations in the upper passages animal life in the cave includes a large population of bats large freshwater crabs crayfish catfish and other tropical fish large invertebrates like amblypygi and various predatory spiders also inhabit the cave agouti and otters may also use the cave these and many other species are quite common in river caves of this size in belize
