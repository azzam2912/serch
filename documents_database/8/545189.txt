originally a separate village weston has become part of bath as the city has grown first through the development of lower weston in victorian times and then by the incorporation of the village into the city with the siting of much local authority housing there in the period after world war ii the ward includes upper weston weston village weston park and the lower slopes of primrose hill the area known as lower weston south of weston road is within kingsmead ward the earliest evidence of occupation comes from two celtic caddy spoons found in the village in 1825 there are believed to have been used as ceremonial anointing regalia during the 10th century weston had been divided into two estates one on the slopes of lansdown was given by edmund i to aethelare in 946 weston was the birthplace of saint alphege who was born around 954 two manors with 41 households are recorded in the domesday book of 1086 one held by bath abbey and the other by arnulf de hesding during the 12th and 13th centuries weston had close ties with the monks of the abbey and in the late 13th century the first vicar of weston
