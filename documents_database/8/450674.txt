the word tabor is simply an english variant of a latin derived word meaning drum cf it has been used in the military as a marching instrument and has been used as accompaniment in parades and processions a tabor has a cylindrical wood shell two skin heads tightened by rope tension a leather strap and an adjustable snare the single snare can be made from gut silk or rough hemp each tabor has a pitch range of about an octave the larger the tabor the lower the pitch it is played by just one short conical stick made from bone or ivory which usually strikes the snare head the tabor is suspended by a strap from the forearm somewhere between the elbow and wrist when played the shell is virtually parallel with the ground in spain a deep drum is used for a tabor by pipe and taborers and in england a shallow tom tom is sometimes used although medieval icons of pipe and tabor usually display a large shallow tabor similar in shape to a bodhr n the tabor is most widely known as accompaniment for the pipe and other small flutes such as the flageolet and most famously
