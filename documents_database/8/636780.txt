the current national assembly has 83 members who are directly elected through a system of party list proportional representation and serve five year terms the first parliament of an independent benin was defined by the constitution of 28 february 1959 and lasted from april 1959 to november 1960 it was chaired by justin ahomadegb tom tin with a 1960 and a 1964 constitution two new national assemblies were enacted each time the implementation of the basic law of 9 september 1977 radically altered the parliament it was renamed the revolutionary national assembly anr and lasted until february 1990 the high council of the republic was formed in february 1990 to democratize the country and was chaired by archbishop isidore de souza a new constitution was passed on 11 december 1990 which formed the basic structure of the current assembly the 83 members of the national assembly are directly elected by the people in a party list proportional representation system with 24 multi member constituencies that are corresponding to the departments of benin parties select as many candidates as there are seats to be filled and vacancies are filled by substitutes they serve five year terms and the most recent election
