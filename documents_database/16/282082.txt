in psychological types jung defines enantiodromia as the emergence of the unconscious opposite in the course of time this characteristic phenomenon practically always occurs when an extreme one sided tendency dominates conscious life in time an equally powerful counterposition is built up which first inhibits the conscious performance and subsequently breaks through the conscious control it is similar to the principle of equilibrium in the natural world in that any extreme is opposed by the system in order to restore balance when things get to their extreme they turn into their opposite however in jungian terms a thing psychically transmogrifies into its shadow opposite in the repression of psychic forces that are thereby cathected into something powerful and threatening this principle was explicitly understood and discussed in the principles of traditional chinese religion as in taoism and yin yang a central premise of the i ching is that yang lines become yin when they have reached their extreme and vice versa the word enantiodromia was apparently coined by stobaeus but the concept is implied also in heraclitus s writings in fr 126 for example heraclitus says cold things warm warm things cool wet things dry and parched things get wet
