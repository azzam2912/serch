the church was built in 1863 to designs of the architect james murray it was consecrated by henry philpott bishop of worcester on thursday 10 december 1863 in 1864 a parish was formed out of the parishes of st peter and st paul aston st silas church lozells and st matthew s church duddeston the church opened a school for girls and infants in 1868 the tower was added in 1882 the church is noted for having a rugby club which played a match against a team from villa cross wesleyan chapel in handsworth for the team from villa cross wesleyan chapel this was their first match as aston villa football club as a condition of the match the villa side had to agree to play the first half under rugby football rules and the second half under association football rules the church was demolished in the 1970s the church was equipped with a two manual pipe organ by norman and beard a specification of the organ can be found on the national pipe organ register but is no longer present
