national forest in mesa delta and garfield counties in western colorado it borders the white river national forest to the north and the gunnison national forest to the east the forest covers most of grand mesa and the south part of battlement mesa it has a total area of 346 555 acres 541 49 sq mi or 1 402 46 km it is managed by the united states forest service together with gunnison national forest and uncompahgre national forest from offices in delta colorado there are local ranger district offices located in grand junction animals that inhabit this forest are elk mule deer canadian lynx black bears pine marten cougars and bighorn sheep birdwatchers get a seasonal opportunity to view species of bird such bald eagles boreal owls golden eagles mexican spotted owls common ravens wild turkeys and peregrine falcons originally called battlement mesa forest reserve created by benjamin harrison on december 24 1892 it was the third forest reserve created in united states it is the largest flat top mountain in the world
