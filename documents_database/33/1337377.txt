after the secession of belgium in 1830 orangist sentiment in flanders and wallonia for a time sought a restoration of the united kingdom of the netherlands it was a movement directed by william i of the netherlands as part of his volhardingspolitiek and containing most of the belgian elites including members of the nobility and industrialists some of the most prominent flemish orangists were jan frans willems and hippolyte metdepenningen although refusing to participate in parliamentary elections as they deemed belgian national institutions to be illegitimate the orangists did take part in local elections at the provincial and municipal levels from which they militated against the new belgian state through political actions and an activist press at least three orangist coups were foiled during the 1830s although losing dutch financial and political support after the treaty of london 1839 and william i s abdication 1840 the weakening belgian orangism survived well into the 1850s strongly opposing the belgian revolution and rallying against independence it was one inspiration for the later greater netherlands movement although that movement was not all monarchist
