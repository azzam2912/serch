it blows in the coastal regions of the south from the outback where the sandy wastes bare of vegetation in summer are intensely heated by the sun this hot wind blows strongly often for several days at a time defying all attempts to keep the dust down and parching all vegetation it is in one sense a healthy wind as being exceedingly dry and hot it destroys many injurious germs the northern brickfielder is almost invariably followed by a strong southerly buster cloudy and cool from the ocean the two winds are due to the same cause viz a cyclonic system over the australian bight these systems frequently extend inland as a narrow v shaped depression the apex northward bringing the winds from the north on their eastern sides and from the south on their western hence as the narrow system passes eastward the wind suddenly changes from north to south and the thermometer has been known to fall in twenty minutes the brickfielder precedes the passage of a frontal zone of a low pressure system passing by and causes severe dust storms that often last for days and led to its naming as the winds blow up red brick
