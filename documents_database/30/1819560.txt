on 16 july 1863 the wesleyan collegiate institute opened with 16 boys and a small number of theological students as no suitable buildings were available in sydney at the time newington house the centrepiece of john blaxland s estate at silverwater was leased the institution functioned as a boys school newington college and theological college expanding student numbers meant that more extensive premises closer to the city were required a bequest by john jones of land at stanmore saw both institutions move to the newly fashionable inner city suburbs seventy schoolboys and four theological students migrated from silverwater to stanmore and the theological college remained there until 1914 the nsw conference of the methodist church purchased a house and grounds at 416 420 liverpool road strathfield south new south wales and moved the theological students there for the beginning of the 1915 academic year the new institution was named leigh college in honour of the revd samuel leigh
