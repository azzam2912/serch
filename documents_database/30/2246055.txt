1634 18 march 1688 styled lord st john of bletsoe from 1642 to 1646 was a british peer and landowner in bedfordshire the eldest son of sir paulet st john d 1638 and his wife elizabeth vaughan he became heir apparent to his grandfather oliver st john 1st earl of bolingbroke after the death of his uncle oliver st john 5th baron st john of bletso at the battle of edgehill in 1642 on 24 november 1654 he married lady frances cavendish d 15 august 1678 the daughter of william cavendish 1st duke of newcastle upon tyne by whom he had no children the st john family were presbyterians and supported parliament during the english civil war but did not take part in government during the interregnum in 1661 he succeeded his relative samuel browne as recorder of bedford and took the oath against the covenant upon becoming a freeman of the town he was appointed custos rotulorum of bedfordshire in 1667 after the death of the 1st earl of cleveland the earl of ailesbury was added as a joint holder of the office in 1671 and bolingbroke was removed from the office by james ii in 1681 bolingbroke died
