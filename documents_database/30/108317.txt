after a course of study in edinburgh he was licensed to preach by the church of scotland but made his way to london in 1771 to teach in schools at edmonton hampstead and camberwell in 1777 he settled as minister of the congregational church at gosport in hampshire his predecessors at the independent chapel of gosport were the rev james watson 1770 76 and the rev thomas williams 1750 70 in 1771 he established an institution for preparing men for the ministry it was the age of the new born missionary enterprise and bogue s academy was largely the seed from which the london missionary society grew in 1800 the society placed missionaries with bogue for preparation for their ministries bogue himself would have gone to india in 1796 if not for the opposition of the east india company in 1824 he taught samuel dyer at gosport before he left for penang as a missionary with the london missionary society he was also involved in founding the british and foreign bible society and the religious tract society and in conjunction with james bennett minister at romsey wrote a well known history of dissenters 3 vols 1809 another of his writings
