pensioner guards were retired army or police officers who were given pay and benefits to travel with convicts on the transportation ships from england upon arrival most of them continued to serve the convict establishment managed from the fremantle prison site as guards land allotments were marked out on the northern portion of toodyay s town site pensioner guard francis kirk was allocated this site and he and his family were the first occupants of the modest cottage that was built as is the case with the nearby hackett s pensioner cottage fmr its original appearance has been modified today the building is at a lower level than the road and is a single storey brick residence with iron hipped roof a side weatherboard extension which extends around to the rear and timber framed windows there is a verandah to the front the brickwork is laid in flemish bond and there is a prominent chimney which has an ornate flue
