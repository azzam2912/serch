it is the largest settlement and the seat of the municipality of sol ava traditionally it belonged to the region of styria and is now included in the savinja statistical region sol ava was attested in written sources as sul pach in 1268 and as sulzpach in 1306 and sulzbach in 1491 in the local dialect the village is called ocpah and in the 19th century its slovene name was recorded as olcpah su pach and solcpah all based on the german name the german name is a compound corresponding to sulze mineral springs bach creek referring to the local geography the modern slovene name sol ava was artificially created in the 19th century from the german name the first half of the name was modified to ol to appear more slovene and the second half was replaced with ava a common suffix associated with streams the parish church in the settlement is dedicated to mary of the snows it dates to the 15th century and contains a 13th century statue of the virgin mary it belongs to the roman catholic diocese of celje
