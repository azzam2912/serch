during the 16th century the abbey played an important political role under its abbess amalberga vos it was a center of religion and charity and was in 1544 given status as a refuge for religious dissidents housing many important people during a time of religious persecution during the great iconoclasm of 1566 the calvinist caspar van der heyden held a speech outside the convent gates the 24 august after which the abbey was attacked the nuns were given safe passage but the abbey was pillaged in 1572 the abbess relocated the nuns to gent and the abbey was de facto no longer active in axel there were plans to return in the 1570s but nothing came of it the same abbey were restored in gent as new ter hage abbey where it was located except for 1578 84 until it was dissolved in 1794
