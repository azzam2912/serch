presumed extinct after 56 years without recorded observations the species was rediscovered in 2015 dysphania pusilla is an annual puberulent herb growing to around in length and often forming a cushion like covering on surfaces such as clay and rocks the herb is distinguishable from other species of the genus such as the introduced dysphania pumilio through the slightly smaller seeds a different number of tepals and the close positioning of the leaves in relation to each other the herb existing in both green and brown colour morphs produces small green and yellow flowers during the warmer months the name is derived from the term pusilla which is the latin word for very small d pusilla a new zealand endemic is generally located in the north eastern corner of the south island of new zealand harry allan in his 1961 mentions that it does not grow south of 43 30 s the plant is usually found in dry sparsely vegetated open spaces including ephemeral wetlands but has also been known to bloom in urban environments such as railway yards and agricultural land the plant was first recorded in 1864 as chenopodium pusillum by joseph dalton hooker in his systematic work
