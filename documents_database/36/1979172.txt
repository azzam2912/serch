traditionally he was recognised as the first person to make practical experiments with steam to propel a vessel but evidence to substantiate the claim that he did more than propose a steam vessel on paper is lacking hulls was born at hanging aston gloucestershire it has been suggested that the background to the efforts of hulls was the 1734 publication in the abridged philosophical transactions of a paper by the french engineer monsieur duquet on ships and mechanical propulsion duquet was a controversialist also active at that time in a debate on his ideas with henri pitot he died in the middle of 1758 in broad campden where he had lived almost all his adult life the protection of his invention by hulls depended on the financial support of his neighbour named freeman at batsford park the patent for the invention by hulls is dated 21 december 1736 and his account of it appeared as description and draught of a new invented machine for carrying vessels or ships out of or into any harbour port or river against wind and tide or in a calm for which his majesty has granted letters patent for the sole benefit of the author
