it is a showy shrub well suited to horticulture distinguished by its very small leaves and small clusters of pink flowers along the older stems melaleuca lateralis is a bushy shrub growing to about tall except on the youngest growth the leaves and branches are glabrous the leaves are arranged alternately around the stem and are long and wide linear to narrow oval in shape roughly semi circular in cross section and usually have a blunt end this species flowers profusely with deep pink flowers in clusters along the stems the clusters contain between 4 and 15 individual flowers and are up to in diameter the petals are long and fall off soon after the flower opens the stamens are arranged in five bundles around the flower each bundle containing 4 to 12 stamens flowering occurs in early spring and is followed by the fruit which are woody capsules long in loose clusters along the stem this species was first described in 1852 by the russian botanist nikolai turczaninow in bulletin de la classe physico mathematique de l academie imperiale des sciences de saint petersburg the specific epithet lateralis is in reference to the inflorescence being inserted on the branchlets
