he is founder director of the early music ensemble sounds baroque and artistic director of cambridge handel opera company julian perkins has been praised for his heartening dramatic energy and dynamic direction while his harpsichord accompaniment has been described as superbly creative and wonderfully adept and stylish his solo discography includes premi res of harpsichord suites by james nares and john christopher smith and clavichord works by herbert howells and stephen dodgson he directed the first recording of daniel purcell s opera oratorio the judgment of paris which he subsequently performed in london at st john s smith square other recordings include johann sebastian bach s french suites on clavichord and wolfgang amadeus mozart s six sonatas for keyboard duet with emma abbate with sounds baroque he has made two recordings of italian cantatas and one of songs by henry purcell with the soprano anna dennis in 2009 julian perkins performed and recorded solo harpsichord at the royal opera house in 2013 julian perkins worked with the academy of ancient music performing in the exhibition about johannes vermeer at london s national gallery julian perkins has conducted staged opera productions for organisations including the buxton festival dutch national opera academy
