the community is located on pennsylvania route 51 at the 2010 census the population was 616 star junction was founded in 1893 when the washington no 2 mine was opened by the washington coal and coke company it is so named because it was once the site of a railroad depot the end of the line for the washington run railroad star junction was once a coal mining center with beehive ovens for coke manufacture and a foundry it was the site of labor unrest including the walkout of 4 500 miners in 1922 although the company store and mines are long gone the patch the groups of company houses still remains and houses many residents this area was added to the determined eligible list of the bureau of historic preservation as an example of a typical coal town and has been added to the national register of historic places john kundla 1916 2017 educator and college professional basketball coach was born in star junction star junction is in northwestern fayette county in the southwest part of perry township via pa 51 it is north to perryopolis and south to uniontown the county seat according to the u s census
