by a letter written on behalf of the secretary of state for the colonies he was informed that his compulsory retirement age as judge was 62 in 1942 malaya was invaded and occupied by japan as a result the secretary of state informed terrell that his service was no longer required and his appointment was terminated on 7 july 1942 17 months before his mandatory retirement age terrell contested the validity of his retirement the case was referred to an arbitrator who found against terrell who appealed to the high court lord goddard found that the termination of terrell s appointment was valid in particular he rejected the contention that terrell could only be removed by the sovereign on presentation of addresses by both houses of parliament pursuant to the procedure set out in section 3 of the act of settlement colonial judges unlike judges of english superior courts held their offices at the sovereign s pleasure and could be dismissed at any time before their mandatory retirement age
