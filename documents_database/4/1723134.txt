split custody is rare as it is thought that it is in the best to keep siblings together for mutual comfort stability and support reasons for split custody can be child preferences for different parents or siblings that do not get along with each other it is more common for older compared to younger siblings and then usually at the request of one of the children split custody is different from shared custody where all children live approximately equal time with each parent in a shared parenting arrangement one criticism of split custody is the same as for sole custody in that the children only have one primary parent which has been shown to cause worse physical mental and social outcomes versus shared parenting compared to sole custody split custody has been criticized for separating siblings and limiting the amount of comfort support and stability that they can give to each other for example older siblings often help younger ones understand what s going on or even shield them from some of the less than pleasant realities
