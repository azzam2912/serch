charpak s family moved from poland to paris when he was seven years old beginning his study of mathematics in 1941 at the lyc e saint louis the actor and film director andr charpak was his brother during world war ii charpak served in the resistance and was imprisoned by vichy authorities in 1943 in 1944 he was deported to the nazi concentration camp at dachau where he remained until the camp was liberated in 1945 after classes pr paratoires studies at lyc e saint louis in paris and later at lyc e joffre in montpellier he joined in 1945 the paris based cole des mines one of the most prestigious engineering schools in france the following year he became a naturalized french citizen he graduated in 1948 earning the french degree of civil engineer of mines ing nieur civil des mines equivalent to a master s degree becoming a pupil in the laboratory of fr d ric joliot curie at the coll ge de france during 1949 the year after curie had directed construction of the first atomic pile within france while at the coll ge charpak secured a research position for the national centre for scientific research cnrs
