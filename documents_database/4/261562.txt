built in square stone style of predominantly grey sandstone the cathedral proper was begun in 1260 and completed in 1501 it stands on the site of the former culdee monastery of dunkeld stones from which can be seen as an irregular reddish streak in the eastern gable it is not formally a cathedral as the church of scotland nowadays has neither cathedrals nor bishops but it is one of a number of similar former cathedrals which has continued to carry the name because of the long construction period the cathedral shows mixed architecture gothic and norman elements are intermingled throughout the structure although partly in ruins the cathedral is in regular use today and is open to the public relics of saint columba including his bones were said to have been kept at dunkeld until the reformation at which time they were removed to ireland some believe there are still undiscovered columban relics buried within the cathedral grounds the original monastery at dunkeld dated from the sixth or early seventh century founded after an expedition of saint columba to the land of alba it was at first a simple collection of wattle huts during the ninth century causant n mac
