woods september 3 1861 december 8 1938 was an american attorney diplomat and politician he was born september 3 1861 in clearfield pennsylvania to matthew woods and catheine katharine bella spice speece he attended lafayette college he later graduated from the university of pennsylvania with a law degree in 1889 woods practiced law in philadelphia and then in pittsburgh where he became associated with the interests of the mellon family on january 18 1893 woods married the former mary todd marchand a great granddaughter of james todd former state attorney general in 1900 woods made his first bid for political office successfully contesting the westmoreland county based 39th district of the pennsylvania state senate he served in the senate for two terms from 1901 to 1907 woods received his first diplomatic appointment in 1912 when president william howard taft named him the united states enovy to portugal with the official title of envoy extraordinary and minister plenipotentiary as the united states had not yet elevated the post to ambassador status in 1915 governor martin brumbaugh appointed him secretary of the commonwealth of pennsylvania woods would serve six years in the post before resigning in 1921 to take up the post
