kitchener is located 5 kilometres south of the town of cessnock nsw and is adjacent to werakata national park and the aberdare state forest the town is named after lord kitchener british secretary for war from july 1914 kitchener was the location of the aberdare central colliery which was developed by caledonian collieries ltd during world war i the historic mine site has been preserved as a heritage park including the poppet head structure a number of the dwellings where originally occupied by mine management in 1914 the mine employed 93 people four years later it employed 287 in july 1943 a large fire caused the mine to close for 12 months the mine was closed in november 1961 the coal holding lease is still retained by coal and allied limited in 2009 a chinese owned mining company austar was granted approval to construct a surface infrastructure site just south of kitchener this facility was built to service the company s longwall top coal caving mining of the greta coal seam in the mine previously known as the southland coal mine south of kitchener
