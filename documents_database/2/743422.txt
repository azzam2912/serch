the chart was first published in the magazine dated july 27 and included ten positions based on reports received from dealers throughout the united states prior to that chart billboard had featured lists of national sheet music best sellers records most popular on music machines compiled from national reports from phonograph operators and songs with the most radio plugs based on a select number of new york city radio stations in the 22 weeks that followed three singles reached the top the first number one song of the chart was i ll never smile again written and produced by canadian pianist ruth lowe in 1939 the version by tommy dorsey and his orchestra featuring vocals by frank sinatra and the vocal group the pied pipers was recorded in april 1940 and released a month later the song topped the chart for twelve consecutive weeks and jumpstarted sinatras career in 1982 the song was included in the grammy hall of fame
