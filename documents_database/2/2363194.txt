the concerto in three movements is an early work by the venetian composer around the mid 1710s johann sebastian bach transcribed the concerto for organ bwv 594 in c major a simplified version of the violin concerto rv 208a without the elaborated cadenzas that appear in manuscript versions of rv 208 and with a different middle movement was published around 1920 in amsterdam as concerto 11 of vivaldi s op 7 vivaldi s violin concerto in d major rv 208 survives in three manuscripts the grosso mogul title appears on the schwerin manuscript which was written before 1717 according to michael talbot the name of the concerto can possibly be linked to domenico lalli s il gran mogol opera libretto a setting of which had been presented in naples in 1713 later settings of this libretto include giovanni porta s staged in venice in 1717 and vivaldi s rv 697 1730 the schwerin and cividale del friuli copies of the concerto contain two variants of extended cadenzas for unaccompanied violin in the first and last movements of the concerto the autograph version indicates where such cadenzas can be inserted in these movements but does not contain the cadenzas a manuscript
