the population was 33 506 at the 2010 census cumberland was originally settled as part of wrentham massachusetts which was purchased from the local indigenous americans by the plymouth colony it was later transferred to rhode island as part of a long running boundary dispute the town was named in honor of prince william duke of cumberland william blackstone also spelled william blaxton in colonial times was the first european to settle and live in cumberland he was also the first european to have settled in boston but left when he and the newly arrived puritans disagreed about religion he preached his brand of tolerant christianity under an oak tree that became an inspiration to christians worldwide he lived on a farm in the lonsdale area of cumberland where he cultivated the first variety of american apples the yellow sweeting the site of his home is now occupied by the ann hope mill the popular tourist destination nine men s misery is a tomb found on the grounds of a former trappist monastery abbey of our lady of the valley part of which was destroyed in a fire in 1950 the trappists sold the monastery and grounds to the town
