hampton glacier discharges tremendous amounts of ice into the head of schokalsky bay at a steep gradient causing the ice there to be extremely broken and irregular and discourages use of this bay and glacier as an inland sledging route onto northeast alexander island the bay was first sighted from a distance in 1909 and roughly charted by the french antarctic expedition under charcot who thinking it to be a strait gave the name detroit schokalsky after yuliy m shokal skiy russian geographer meteorologist and oceanographer charcot followed the spelling schokalsky used by the man himself when writing in roman script the coast in this vicinity was photographed from the air and this bay roughly charted in 1937 by the british graham land expedition but charcot s detroit schokalsky was not identified surveys by falkland islands dependencies survey in 1948 identified this bay as the feature originally named by charcot
