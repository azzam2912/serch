after serving in world war ii he attended an agricultural college and established a dairy farm in hampshire as demand in britain rose for meat roberts saw increasing use of factory farming which had been imported from the united states encouraged by popular support for his letter writing campaign roberts unsuccessfully attempted to convince existing animal welfare organizations to protest intensive farming as a result he founded compassion in world farming in 1967 he gave up dairy farming the same year and he established a health food shop in petersfield in 1978 he led the organization until 1991 when he retired and was replaced by joyce d silva although roberts was a vegetarian he did not push for others to change their eating habits he was an advocate of reasoned debate and sought change through peaceful means he was awarded an mbe in 2002 for his work associated with compassion in world farming
