pursued by the amorous god pan she ran to a river s edge and asked for assistance from the river nymphs in answer she was transformed into hollow water reeds that made a haunting sound when the god s frustrated breath blew across them pan cut the reeds to fashion the first set of pan pipes which were thenceforth known as syrinx the word syringe was derived from this word the story of the syrinx is told in achilles tatius leukippe and kleitophon where the heroine is subjected to a virginity test by entering a cave where pan has left syrinx pipes that will sound a melody if she passes the story became popular among artists and writers in the 19th century the victorian artist and poet thomas woolner wrote silenus a long narrative poem about the myth in which syrinx becomes the lover of silenus but drowns when she attempts to escape rape by pan as a result of the crime pan is transmuted into a demon figure and silenus becomes a drunkard amy clampitt s poem syrinx refers to the myth by relating the whispering of the reeds to the difficulties of language longus makes reference to syrinx
