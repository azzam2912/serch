it was known to fruit growers of the late 19th and early 20th centuries as a mortgage lifter because it was a reliable producer and the fruit would not drop from the trees until very late in the season by mid twentieth century it was mostly used as a process apple rather than a table apple and orchards were replacing it with more popular varieties the cultivar is now very rare to nonexistent in the commercial trade it is still grown in parts of california and maine the ben davis was crossbred with the mcintosh to create the cortland which has been a very successful pie apple similar cultivars known as gano or black ben davis appeared in parts of the american south notably arkansas and virginia in the 1880s they are said to be either seedlings of or identical to the original ben davis but the exact relationship is unknown
