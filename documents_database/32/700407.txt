it is one of the most popular tourist destinations on the island as well as a gathering place for locals the beach sits at the head of a deep bay the arms of which are peterborg peninsula to the east and tropaco point to the west the bay s northwest exposure means its waters are usually calm although storms in the north atlantic can occasionally generate large waves in the winter months the sandy bottom means snorkeling is of limited interest although turtles conch tarpon and other fish are commonly spotted local fishing boats anchor in the eastern part of the bay the beach and surrounding park are currently administered by the semi autonomous magens bay authority the services provided by the authority include lifeguards parking showers and bathrooms there is a snack counter bar and souvenir shop beach chairs floats lounge chairs mask and snorkels fins towels lockers paddleboards kayaks and paddleboats can be rented the authority is a self sustaining entity which funds its operations through a per person entrance fee currently 5 00 for visitors and 2 00 for locals free for kids under 12 parking fees and payments received from the park s restaurant and rental
