the low square shaped tower was part of the original building the romanesque doorway in the southern wall is composed of three archivolts and decorated capitals next to this entrance stands the bronze sculpture 2012 by ricardo gonzalez gil representing leonor izquierdo cuevas dressed in the fashion of her time the interior of the church is composed of a nave covered by a ribbed vault and two aisles covered by barrel vaults the walnut choir dates from 1523 its origin is uncertain and probably originated from the collegiate church of san pedro or from the abbey of la merced the massive altarpiece is the work of francisco de agreda 1571 with influences of juan de juni its style shows a transition between the plateresque style and mannerism much of the church was rebuilt in the 19th century when it threatened to become a ruin the church was declared a national monument in 1929
