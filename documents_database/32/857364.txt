the piece is built around several distinct themes the first appears in the woodwinds and strings the second is presented by the english horn while the third is scored for violas against figures and chords in the woodwinds and lower strings this is worked out vigorously before the piece is concluded by a new theme first from the violins and repeated by the woodwinds the published score of the fantasy contains two quotations which provide some clue as to its emotional content the first is a couplet by dante gabriel rossetti the origin of the second quote is unknown it reads roses lilies and a thousand scented flowers bright butterflies flitting from petal to petal beneath the shade of ancient trees a quiet river with water lilies in a boat almost hidden two people a thrush is singing in the distance the first broadcast performance was conducted by edward clark philip heseltine made a piano solo transcription in 1921 published only in 1982
