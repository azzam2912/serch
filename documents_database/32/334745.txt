the main branch of the library sits at the rear of the centre block on parliament hill in ottawa ontario and is the last untouched part of that larger building s original incarnation after it burned down in 1916 the library has been augmented and renovated a number of times since its construction in 1876 the last between 2002 and 2006 though the form and decor remain essentially authentic the building today serves as a canadian icon and appears on the obverse of the canadian ten dollar bill the library is overseen by the parliamentary librarian of canada and an associate or assistant librarian the canadian parliamentary poet laureate is considered to be an officer of the library designed by thomas fuller and chilion jones and inspired by the british museum reading room the building is formed as a chapter house separated from the main body of the centre block by a corridor this arrangement as well as many other details of the design was reached with the input of the then parliamentary librarian alpheus todd the walls supported by a ring of 16 flying buttresses are load bearing double wythe masonry consisting of a hydraulic lime rubble fill core
