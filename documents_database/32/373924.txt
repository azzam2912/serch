the first segment also known as the west coast highway connects cape town with velddrif along the west coast the second runs from vredendal via vanrhynsdorp calvinia brandvlei and kenhardt to keimoes on the n14 near upington the connection between velddrif and vredendal has never been built although it can be driven on various gravel roads originally the r27 designation continued beyond upington all the way to pretoria but this section became part of the n14 and various other roads in cape town the highway connects with table bay boulevard n1 near the table bay harbor and then runs north along the coast for stretch that is known as marine drive this stretch contains many industrial warehouses later the road moves further inland and the m14 becomes the main coastal road further north the road passes through west coast national park before reaching velddrif the northern segment of the road is exceptionally desolate it is a distance of from calvinia to keimoes and the only towns along the way are brandvlei and kenhardt
