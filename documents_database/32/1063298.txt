the estate is noted as being one of the finest examples of agro industrial history on saint kitts and contains the only surviving cotton ginnery on the island the estate began as an animal driven sugar mill estate possible around the late 17th century and converted to steam power in the late 19th century around 1900 its ownership changed hands to sendall and wade the new owners made history in the caribbean as in 1901 they installed the first cotton ginnery on st kitts and made spooner s estate one of the caribbean s first to successfully convert from the sugar cane industry to cotton production the estate s remains on the site today include the structures and ruins of agro industrial technology from the 17th 19th and 20th centuries they represent three major periods of agricultural history in saint kitts and nevis no official restoration project or park designation of the spooner s estate area has occurred to date
