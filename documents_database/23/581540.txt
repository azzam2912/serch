although principally associated with the irish army defence force regulations in fact describe it as the army badge it is also worn by and appears in elements of the insignia of the naval service and air corps the badge was designed in 1913 by eoin macneill a founding member and chairman of the irish volunteers variations existed for territorial commands but the majority of volunteers wore the glaigh na h ireann badge it was worn by republicans in the 1916 easter rising it was rarely worn by the irish republican army in the war of independence as doing so could lead to a prison term eventually the free state army adopted the badge for their new uniforms before the irish civil war the design of the army badge which is prescribed in defence force regulations as follows in the army the badge is worn by all ranks on all head dress enlisted and non commissioned ranks wear a stay brite anodised aluminium brass replica some enlisted ranks particularly older soldiers wear the original brass badge which although no longer official issue is considered a symbol of lengthy service commissioned officers and senior ncos such as sergeant major and battalion regimental
