the population was 4 751 at the 2010 census it includes the villages of east warren warren and south warren the latter home to the maine state prison and minimum security bolduc correctional facility part of the waldo patent it was called the upper town of st georges plantation it was first settled by scots irish settlers from londonderry in 1736 under the auspices of brigadier general samuel waldo its proprietor development was hindered however by the ongoing french and indian wars in 1753 a blockhouse was built and placed under the command of captain thomas kilpatrick known by terrified indians as tom kill the devil war raged across maine between 1754 1758 and local settlers took refuge in the blockhouse or at another in cushing hostilities ended in 1759 with the fall of quebec on november 7 1776 upper town of st georges plantation was incorporated as a town named after joseph warren a revolutionary war hero hay was the principal crop shipbuilding was an important industry and between 1770 1850 224 vessels were built varying from 53 to 1 127 tons warren had quarries to extract granite and limestone falls on the st george river provided water power
