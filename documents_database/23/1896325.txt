like all members of the family cyclopigidae it has very large convex eyes that cover most of the free cheeks and in some species touch each other the eyes almost touch the large glabella the occipital ring has merged with the rest of the glabella the glabella does not extend into a frontal thorn the cephalon lacks genal spines the 6 thorax segments have short pleurae the pygidium is rather large and often rather effaced these are features that also occur in other cyclopygidae and are indications of a pelagic lifestyle the genus was first named egle by barrande but that name was not available since it was used by robineau desvoidy before for a willow catkin fly of the anthomyiidae family as pelagic creatures cyclogygids were not bound to shallow waters surrounding continents and islands at the time they lived continents weren t blocking the exchange between different parts of the ocean and this means they probably had large distributions comprising one or more climate zones worldwide cyclopyge is well known from the czech republic morocco england sweden canada newfoundland and the united states
