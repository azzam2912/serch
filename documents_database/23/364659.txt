he later changed his family name from esp rito santo to the more aristocratic sequeira he studied art first at the academy of lisbon before moving to rome where he was antonio cavallucci s pupil by the age of thirteen he had evinced such marked talent that f de setubal employed him as an assistant in his work for the jo o ferreiras palace sequeira resided in rome from 1788 to 1794 when he was made honorary member of the academy of st luke after another two years and further study in italy he returned to his native country with such a great reputation that important commissions for churches and palaces were immediately entrusted to him scriptural subjects large historical compositions and cabinet pictures in 1802 he was appointed first court painter and in this role executed many works for the prince regent for dona maria teresa and for members of the court he designed the valuable silver service which was presented by the portuguese nation to wellington and a monument that was erected in 1820 in the rossio square at lisbon in 1823 he visited paris where he is known to have tried his skill in lithography and etching
