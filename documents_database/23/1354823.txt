he entered private practice in cannelton indiana from 1827 to 1830 he was a prosecutor for the seventh judicial circuit of indiana from 1830 to 1832 he was a member of the indiana house of representatives from 1832 to 1836 he resumed private practice in vigo county indiana from 1834 to 1837 he was presiding judge of the indiana circuit court for the seventh judicial circuit from 1837 to 1841 he was commissioner of the united states general land office in washington d c from 1841 to 1842 huntington was nominated by president john tyler on april 26 1842 to a seat on the united states district court for the district of indiana vacated by judge jesse lynch holman he was confirmed by the united states senate on may 2 1842 and received his commission the same day his service terminated on october 26 1862 due to his death in saint paul minnesota
