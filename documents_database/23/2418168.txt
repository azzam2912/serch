fisher peak is the highest point in the fisher range so its nearest higher peak is mount evan thomas to the southwest in the opal range precipitation runoff from the mountain drains west into evan thomas creek which is a tributary of the kananaskis river or east into the little elbow river which is a tributary of the elbow river the peak was named in 1920 after john arbuthnot fisher 1st baron fisher a british admiral known for reforming and modernizing the royal navy prior to world war i jackie fisher as he was commonly known has been considered the second most important person in british naval history the mountain s name was officially adopted by the geographical names board of canada in 1924 the first ascent of the mountain was made in 1950 by j f tarrant fisher peak is composed of sedimentary rock laid down during the precambrian to jurassic periods formed in shallow seas this sedimentary rock was pushed east and over the top of younger rock during the laramide orogeny based on the k ppen climate classification fisher peak is located in a subarctic climate with cold snowy winters and mild summers temperatures can drop below
