most of the instruments of the lyre family are played by plucking the strings but some involve the use of a bow instead the sound box can be either bowl shaped 321 21 or box shaped 321 22 in the first case the resonator is often a turtle shell while the sound board is made of leather in the second case usually both the body and the sound board are made of wood examples of yoke lutes are the lyre the cithara and the phorminx from ancient greece the biblical kinnor and the african nyatiti however there are other instruments called lyra or lira that do not belong from an organological point of view to this family but rather to the handle lutes such as the byzantine lyra the calabrian lira the cretan lyra the lira da braccio the lyra viol
