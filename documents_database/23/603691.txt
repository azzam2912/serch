these are irregular isolated bones that can appear in addition to the usual centres of ossification of the skull and although unusual are not rare they occur most frequently in the course of the lambdoid suture which is more tortuous than other sutures they are also occasionally seen within the sagittal and coronal sutures a large wormian bone at lambda is often called an inca bone os incae due to the relatively high frequency of occurrence in peruvian mummies another specific wormian bone the pterion ossicle sometimes exists between the sphenoidal angle of the parietal bone and the great wing of the sphenoid bone they tend to vary in size and can be found on either side of the skull usually not more than several are found in a single individual but more than one hundred have been once found in the skull of a hydrocephalic adult wormian bones are a marker for some diseases and important in the primary diagnosis of brittle bone disease osteogenesis imperfecta wormian bones may also be seen in these causes can be remembered by the mnemonic porkchops wormian bones are named for ole worm professor of anatomy at copenhagen 1588 1654 he taught latin
