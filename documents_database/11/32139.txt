in both the poetic edda compiled in the 13th century from earlier traditional sources and the prose edda written in the 13th century by snorri sturluson n tt is listed as the daughter of a figure by the name of n rvi with variant spellings and is associated with the horse hr mfaxi while the prose edda features information about n tt s ancestry including her three marriages n tt s third marriage was to the god dellingr and this resulted in their son dagr the personified day although some manuscript variations list j r as dellingr s wife and dagr s mother instead as a proper noun the word n tt appears throughout old norse literature in stanza 24 of the poem vaf r nism l the god odin disguised as gagnr r asks the j tunn vaf r nir from where the day comes and the night and its tides in stanza 25 vaf r nir responds in stanza 14 of the vaf r nism l odin states that the horse hr mfaxi draws every night to the beneficent gods and that he lets foam from his bit fall every morning from which dew comes to the valleys
