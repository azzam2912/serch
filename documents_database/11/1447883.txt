in 1932 it was joined by the amalgamated society of tailors and tailoresses and renamed itself as the national union of tailors and garment workers in 1939 it absorbed the united ladies tailors trade union the nutgw had 118 700 members in 1945 making it the tenth largest union in britain it absorbed the manchester based waterproof garment workers trade union in 1972 the nutgw faced a long term decline in membership over the second half of the 20th century as the number of workers employed in the british clothing industry shrunk this was largely due to competition with foreign manufacturers by 1990 membership had fallen to less than 70 000 from a peak of over 130 000 in 1950 the nutgw merged into the general municipal boilermakers and allied trades union in 1991 the union sponsored a labour party member of parliament from 1958 until 1964
