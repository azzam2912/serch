vilas was admitted to the vermont bar in 1833 and practiced law he was a member of the 1835 vermont constitutional convention in 1836 he served in the vermont house of representatives and in 1840 was elected to the vermont state senate he was also elected probate judge in 1851 vilas and his family moved to madison wisconsin there vilas was elected mayor of the city he started a law practice in wisconsin from which the present day law firm of bell moore richter s c draws its roots vilas also served in the wisconsin state assembly in 1855 1868 and 1873 during the american civil war vilas was a draft commissioner he was one of the regents of the university of wisconsin system and the wisconsin historical society his son was william freeman vilas who served in the united states senate his former home in madison is located in what is now the langdon street historic district
