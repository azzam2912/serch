created by an andesitic lava flow approximately seven million years ago and shaped by erosion they now stand about above the surrounding rogue valley the table rocks are jointly owned the nature conservancy is responsible for while the bureau of land management is responsible for native americans have inhabited the table rocks area for at least 15 000 years before european american settlement starting in the mid 19th century during a gold rush the settlers forced the takelma tribe away from the table rocks and into reservations the surrounding area was quickly developed the table rock post office was established in 1872 an airstrip was built atop lower table rock in 1948 and a very high frequency omni directional range vor aviation tower was constructed on upper table rock in the 1960s the table rocks were not protected until the 1970s the rocks are home to over 70 species of animals and 340 species of plants which includes over 200 species of wildflowers vernal pools atop the plateaus fill during the rainy season in winter and spring because the andesite is impermeable the dwarf woolly meadowfoam a species of wildflower grows around these pools and is endemic to the rocks
