the measurement data are processed directly in the station at the foot of the tower and then transferred to the institute of forest in krasnoyarsk russia as well as to the max planck institute for biogeochemistry in jena germany the station has been operational since september 2006 it extended the project terrestrial carbon observing system and was funded by the 5th framework programme of the european union uniting 8 european and 4 russian partners a main conclusion of the project is that siberian forests constitute a substantially smaller carbon sink than so far assumed
