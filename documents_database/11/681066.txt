it is an art form from where beautiful and detailed figurines are created from a bread dough mixture these figurines can be anything from fruits animals people ornaments nativity scenes etc masap n literally translates into masa dough p n bread women in and around the region of calder n ecuador have been making dough art for generations as a holiday tradition the recipe for the mixture is simple it is a combination of wheat flour white glue and water this recipe varies from family to family and is kept secret as it is passed down from generation to generation beginning around 1940 they began selling their figurines in local shops the dough is hand rolled and sculpted with small tools knives and toothpicks the sharp points of the tools are ideal for the details the figurines are known for they are then set to air dry for days or weeks before they are meticulously painted a skilled artisan can make about 300 figurines in one day in a month a shop typically makes about 20 000 prices range between a quarter to 10 depending on the size they are sold year round but are extremely popular on november 2
