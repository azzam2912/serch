the avifauna of the cayman islands included a total of 259 species according to bird checklists of the world as of june 2019 of them 155 are rare or accidental and nine are introduced one species grand cayman thrush was endemic to the islands but is now extinct and one species jamaican oriole has been extirpated one additional introduced species has been added through ebird birdlife international has reclassified grand cayman s endemic cuban bullfinch subspecies as a full species the grand cayman bullfinch but clements has not yet accepted this in addition the islands hold most of the world population of vitelline warbler which otherwise occurs only on the swan islands in honduras there are 17 endemic subspecies belonging to 14 different species this list s taxonomic treatment designation and sequence of orders families and species and nomenclature english and scientific names are those of the clements checklist of birds of the world 2019 edition the following tags have been used to highlight several categories of occurrence order anseriformesfamily anatidae anatidae includes the ducks and most duck like waterfowl such as geese and swans these birds are adapted to an aquatic existence with webbed feet flattened bills and feathers
