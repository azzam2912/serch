rare but widely distributed it is found in the indo pacific from mozambique to india to northern australia this species may be semi pelagic in nature inhabiting both deeper waters and shallow coastal reefs and estuaries it is characterized by a diamond shaped pectoral fin disc much wider than long a tail that is broad and flattened in front of the spine but whip like behind and large white spots over its back the very wide shape of the smalleye stingray differs from that of most other members of its family and may reflect a mode of swimming similar to other rays such as manta rays this species is aplacental viviparous with litter sizes perhaps as small as one pup a handful of smalleye stingrays are caught incidentally by commercial fisheries across its range at present there is insufficient data for the international union for conservation of nature iucn to determine its conservation status beyond data deficient in 1908 scottish zoologist nelson annandale described the smalleye stingray as trygon microps in the second volume of records of the indian museum he based his account on a large specimen collected from a depth of in the bay of bengal near chittagong
