as summarised by j n yngvi j hannsson bankster is in most respects a well written traditional psychological realist novel and a good example of the genre it tells the story of an individual without attempting to any significant degree to put it in a wider or more general context mark s is a young person who has worked in a bank he was doing well before the crash and seemed absolutely typical of his section of society he sails more or less unknowingly to his doom just as one imagines the majority of ordinary bank employees have along with his wife he is in a good position to continue his life well educated with almost no debts and doesn t have much to complain about but what eventuates on the other hand is neither a new life nor a development of the old but rather a familiar downward spiral the lack of purpose and activity bit by bit take over mark s s life the only thing he does is to write by himself in the diary that his friend a historian with an interest in preserving sources about the present gives him but writing provides him neither with
