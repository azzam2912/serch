theologian was not applied to symeon in the modern academic sense of theological study the title was designed only to recognize someone who spoke from personal experience of the vision of god one of his principal teachings was that humans could and should experience theoria literally contemplation or direct experience of god symeon was born into the byzantine nobility and given a traditional education at age fourteen he met symeon the studite a renowned monk of the monastery of stoudios in constantinople who convinced him to give his own life to prayer and asceticism under the elder symeon s guidance by the time he was thirty symeon the new theologian became the abbot of the monastery of st mammas a position he held for twenty five years he attracted many monks and clergy with his reputation for sanctity though his teachings brought him into conflict with church authorities who would eventually send him into exile his most well known disciple was nicetas stethatos who wrote the life of symeon symeon is recognized as the first byzantine mystic to freely share his own mystical experiences some of his writings are included in the philokalia a collection of texts by early christian
