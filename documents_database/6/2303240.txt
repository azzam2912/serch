a 1582 source reported that news of the work had circulated in spain and in rome and that philip ii of spain and alfonso ii d este had both tried in vain to buy it the milanese nobleman pirro visconti succeeded in buying it around 1587 and brought it to his villa at lainate in milan where it found favour as part of the deeply religious era of st charles borromeo who kept a copy of it in the room where he died it was also copied by giulio campi in a painting now in the pinacoteca ambrosiana from the house of visconti it passed to the marquis of caracena before being taken to spain by the end of the 17th century there it was admired by anton raphael mengs before being acquired by the duke of wellington during the peninsular war
