she maintained a contact with eudoxia lopukhina after lopukhina had been placed in a convent and passed messages and money between lopukhina and her son alexei petrovich when alexei petrovich left russia she met him on the way and brought a letter from him to his mother after the arrest of alexei maria was taken in custody accused of having assisted his escape she was imprisoned first in prison and then in house arrest in saint petersburg she was released in 1721 maria alexeyevna outlived all of her sisters and died in 1723 two years before the death of her brother she was buried in the saints peter and paul cathedral saint petersburg
