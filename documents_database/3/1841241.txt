it is a typical dish of szczecin where it was popular during the time of the polish people s republic and still retains this popularity having become a cultural food of the region the filling consists of either minced beef the oldest and the most popular or sauerkraut and dried mushrooms or cheese and champignons during the time of the ppr when a lack of meat on the market was a frequent occurrence it was common to replace the meat stuffing with egg paste the dough is crispy on the outside and soft inside the minced beef filling resembles p t the polish word pasztecik is a diminutive of the word pasztet p t usually served with clear spicy red barszcz it should not be frozen or warmed again the first bar serving pasztecik szczeci ski bar pasztecik still functioning was founded in 1969 using machines imported from the soviet union army stationing in szczecin which could quickly produce large amounts of food for the soviet soldiers the machine weighing over one tonne is able to produce over 600 pasztecik in an hour from the 22 december 2010 pasztecik szczeci ski is listed on the official polish traditional products list
