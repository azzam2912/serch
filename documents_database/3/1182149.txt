this is the last historic reference to the castle before the manor house was built manchester castle may have taken the form of a ringwork constructed from timber and with a wooden palisade this earlier castle has been described as of no political or military importance three rings of ditches have been discovered surrounding the likely site of the castle however these may be part of a saxon burh or norman castle in his book warfare in england 1912 author and historian hilaire belloc identified the manchester gap between the pennines and the mersey estuary to be one of the two most important defensive lines in medieval england along with the line of the river thames although belloc ascribed great importance to manchester and its notional ability to hamper troop movements castle historian d j cathcart king refuted belloc as the site was forgotten at an early date
