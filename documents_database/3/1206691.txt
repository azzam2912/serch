the diocese of derby archives record that there was an ancient chapel in the village but no traces of that remain a school known as st mary s mission and school house were built in the 1860s but both these are now residential there are three pre historic rock shelters behind the former school on magg lane and opposite the pub the black horse these are linked to the creswell crags scarcliffe park an area of woodland to the south end of the village has bronze age and roman remains it is surrounded by a pale ditch the village is a conservation area and has an active residents association the majority of the buildings are constructed from the local limestone the waterworks on whaley moor featured in channel 4 series grand designs whaley most likely comes from the celtic word whallis meaning water a tributary of the river poulter runs through the village and there are a number of local springs a well now capped was situated near red brick cottage
