the scottish great highland bagpipes are the best known in the anglophone world however bagpipes have been played for a millennium or more throughout large parts of europe northern africa and western asia including anatolia the caucasus and around the persian gulf the term bagpipe is equally correct in the singular or plural though pipers usually refer to the bagpipes as the pipes a set of pipes or a stand of pipes a set of bagpipes minimally consists of an air supply a bag a chanter and usually at least one drone many bagpipes have more than one drone and sometimes more than one chanter in various combinations held in place in stocks sockets that fasten the various pipes to the bag the most common method of supplying air to the bag is through blowing into a blowpipe or blowstick in some pipes the player must cover the tip of the blowpipe with their tongue while inhaling but most blowpipes have a non return valve that eliminates this need in recent times there are many instruments that assist in creating a clean air flow to the pipes and assist the collection of condensation an innovation dating from the 16th or
