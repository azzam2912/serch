at age 12 he was apprenticed to a cabinetmaker for nine years he purchased his contract after six years after graduating from high school he taught school and read law in 1846 he entered private practice in springfield ohio from 1846 to 1852 he was elected prosecutor for clark county ohio in 1847 and reelected three times serving from 1847 to 1856 he was a judge of the clark county court of common pleas from 1856 to 1864 when justice hocking h hunter resigned february 9 1864 from the supreme court of ohio white was appointed to the seat the next day he won election to the remainder of the term in 1864 and won re election to five year terms in 1868 1873 and 1878 ultimately serving on the supreme court until his death white was nominated by president chester a arthur on february 9 1883 to a seat on the united states district court for the southern district of ohio vacated by judge philip bergen swing he was confirmed by the united states senate on february 19 1883 and received his commission the same day his service terminated on march 12 1883 due to his death in springfield
