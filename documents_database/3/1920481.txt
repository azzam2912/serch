the station opened on 1 november 1956 it closed in 1987 when the entire line closed just before privatization of japanese national railways jnr the station become famous after it was featured on a japanese nhk tv travel documentary programme in 1973 in fiscal 2012 it was visited by about 175 000 people making it one of the top tourist attractions in the area the ageing station structure was scheduled to be renovated by obihiro city government between september and november 2013 at a cost of 33 million yen the station takes its name from the name of the area in which it is located although the area was originally called k shin it was changed to k fuku in around 1910 by combining the k of k shin with fuku from fukui denoting the farmers from fukui prefecture who moved to the area around 1902 after being displaced by flooding
