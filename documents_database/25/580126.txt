aqueduct water supplied public baths latrines fountains and private households it also supported mining operations milling farms and gardens aqueducts moved water through gravity alone along a slight overall downward gradient within conduits of stone brick or concrete the steeper the gradient the faster the flow most conduits were buried beneath the ground and followed the contours of the terrain obstructing peaks were circumvented or less often tunneled through where valleys or lowlands intervened the conduit was carried on bridgework or its contents fed into high pressure lead ceramic or stone pipes and siphoned across most aqueduct systems included sedimentation tanks which helped to reduce any water borne debris sluices and castella aquae distribution tanks regulated the supply to individual destinations in cities and towns the run off water from aqueducts scoured the drains and sewers rome s first aqueduct was built in 312 bc and supplied a water fountain at the city s cattle market by the 3rd century ad the city had eleven aqueducts sustaining a population of over a million in a water extravagant economy most of the water supplied the city s many public baths cities and towns throughout the roman empire emulated this model and
