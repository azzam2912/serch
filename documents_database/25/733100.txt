its natural distribution covers mexico guatemala belize honduras el salvador nicaragua costa rica panama colombia ecuador peru bolivia paraguay the amazon basin brazil and french guiana this species often has multiple trunks which may be 15 m tall and about 10 cm in diameter they are covered in black spines and together form a medium sized feathery clump fronds are bipinnate and 2 3 m long the rachis and stipe are brown to dark brown and are covered with scales the scales are bicoloured having a dark brown to blackish centre and a pale whitish margin pinnule veins sometimes have small brown star shaped scales sori are round and form on either side of the pinnule midvein they are covered by globose indusia in cultivation a cuspidata should be provided with high humidity and warm temperatures it should not be exposed to frost
