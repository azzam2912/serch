the second named storm of the 1971 atlantic hurricane season beth s genesis was impeded by a cold environment surrounding the storm however as it continued on its northeastward course it achieved fully tropical characteristics and peaked as a modest category 1 hurricane on the saffir simpson hurricane scale the cyclone struck nova scotia on august 16 before dissipating shortly after beth produced substantial precipitation and the resultant flooding inflicted severe damage to infrastructure and crops on august 9 a low pressure system in the upper levels of the atmosphere formed off the east coast of florida it became a surface low by the next day and acquired the characteristics of a tropical cyclone it is officially stated to have become a tropical depression at 1200 utc on august 10 the depression slowly advanced northeastward within a relatively cold environment and as such it did not intensify for several days the system turned more toward the east as it approached the coast of north carolina at which time its structure became conducive to further development on august 14 the depression was declared a tropical storm and assigned the name beth following recent data from a reconnaissance aircraft several hundred miles
