he was commissioned as a second lieutenant on 3 october 1914 having previously been a cadet serjeant in the senior division of the oxford university contingent of the officers training corps he served in the royal air force during the first world war and was a member of the scientific staff at netheravon in 1922 whiston joined the chemistry department of the royal military college of science becoming associate professor in 1936 after the second world war he became associate professor of applied chemistry a new post he was also active in the work of the institution of professional civil servants he specialized in the study of service explosives he published papers with amongst others david chapman the chemistry fellow at jesus college he was made an officer of the order of the british empire his obituary in the times on 5 march 1956 noted that he had died recently at the age of 63
