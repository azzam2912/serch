during his youth he was a successful cricketer with hampshire and new south wales he graduated from christ church oxford with a b a he studied law at the inner temple and was called to the bar in 1872 he served as a justice of the peace for hampshire he was elected to the house of commons for basingstoke in 1887 a seat he held until his death he was sworn a member of the imperial privy council on 11 august 1902 following an announcement of the king s intention to make this appointment in the 1902 coronation honours list published in june that year he served briefly under arthur balfour as parliamentary secretary to the local government board from june to december 1905 jeffreys died in february 1906 aged 57 in 1877 he married amy fenwick and their son george became a prominent military commander and was elevated to the peerage as baron jeffreys in 1952
