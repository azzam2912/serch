this phrase is often used to describe a person who acts as a locum tenens placeholder in the absence of a superior such as the president pro tempore of the united states senate who acts in place of the president of the united states senate the vice president of the united states legislative bodies can have one or more pro tempore for the presiding officer these positions ostensibly go to legislators experienced in floor debate who are familiar with the content and application of relevant rules and precedents and who have a reputation for fairness among their colleagues a common use of pro tempore in the united states is in municipalities such as cities and towns with regard to the position of the mayor some cities do not have a position of vice mayor but rather appoint a person from the city council to act as mayor pro tempore pro tem in the absence of the actual mayor in judicial courts attorneys that volunteer in proceedings are called judge pro tem
