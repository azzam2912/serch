the native name is kala a al kalasha language waigali refers to the dialect of the v y people of the upper part of the waigal valley centered on the town of waigal which is distinct from the dialect of the ima ni ei people who inhabit the lower valley the word kalasha is the native ethnonym for all the speakers of the southern nuristani languages kala a al belongs to the indo european language family and is in the southern nuristani group of the indo iranian branch it is closely related to zemiaki and to tregami the lexical similarity with the latter being approximately 76 to 80 it shares its name with kala a mun spoken in pakistan s southern chitral district but the two languages belong to different branches of indo iranian waigali speakers are sometimes called red kalasha while the speakers of the language in pakistan are called black kalasha according to linguist richard strand the kalasha of chitral apparently adopted the name of the nuristani kalasha who at some unknown time had extended their influence into the region of southern chitral
