the mountain is covered by and surrounded with a 345 km forested area on ultrabasic rocks formerly known in indonesian as hutan querelau lauberio it is characterised by a somewhat stunted vegetation of what was originally semi evergreen monsoon forest now degraded and mainly confined to the higher ridges with eucalypt woodland subject to grazing elsewhere the site has been identified by birdlife international as an important bird area iba because it supports populations of bar necked cuckoo doves timor green pigeons pink headed imperial pigeons yellow crested cockatoos olive headed lorikeets jonquil parrots timor friarbirds flame eared honeyeaters plain gerygones olive brown orioles timor stubtails timor leaf warblers spot breasted heleias chestnut backed thrushes and orange sided thrushes
