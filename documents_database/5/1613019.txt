the flight was performed under a funded agreement from nasa as the second dragon demonstration mission in the commercial orbital transportation services cots program the purpose of the cots program is to develop and demonstrate commercial sources for cargo re supply of the international space station iss the dragon c2 spacecraft was the first american vehicle to visit the iss since the end of the space shuttle program it was also the first commercial spacecraft to rendezvous and berth with another spacecraft initially the objectives of the c2 mission were to have been accomplished by two separate missions dragon c2 would have carried out a fly by of the iss practiced rendezvous maneuvers and communications with the station before returning to earth a second mission c3 would have been the first mission to berth with the station in july 2011 nasa gave tentative approval to combine the objectives of the two missions in december 2011 nasa formally approved the merger of the cots 2 and 3 missions into the dragon c2 flight there were several launch delays the last one occurring on 19 may 2012 due to a launch abort during the last second before liftoff dragon c2 successfully launched
