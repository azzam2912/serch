the term art game was first used academically in 2002 and it has come to be understood as describing a video game designed to emphasize art or whose structure is intended to produce some kind of reaction in its audience art games are interactive usually competitive against the computer self or other players and the result of artistic intent by the party offering the piece for consideration they also typically go out of their way to have a unique unconventional look often standing out for aesthetic beauty or complexity in design the concept has been extended by some art theorists to the realm of modified modded gaming when modifications have been made to existing non art games to produce graphic results intended to be viewed as an artistic display as opposed to modifications intended to change game play scenarios or for storytelling modified games created for artistic purposes are sometimes referred to as video game art art games are often considered a means of demonstrating video games as works of art a definition of the art game was first proposed by professor tiffany holmes school of the art institute of chicago in her 2003 paper for the melbourne dac conference
