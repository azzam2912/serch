over 100 species have been recorded including many rare and scarce species this section of coast features an almost continuous 5 km stretch of soft head deposits on a raised hard rock platform these cliffs are south facing and feature sheltered coves offering ideal conditions for thermophilic warmth loving invertebrates the friable cliff material and high temperatures attract large nesting aggregations of solitary bees and wasps which burrow into the cliff prawle cliffs are the only site in the uk for the rare cuckoo bee nomada sexfasciata this is a cleptoparasite of the long horned mining bee eucera longicornis another rare species of particular note is the mason wasp euodynerus quadrifasciatus currently this is only known from three sites in the uk east prawle west weare portland dorset and thursley common surrey prawle is also an important site for the cirl bunting and supports a rich lichen assemblage
