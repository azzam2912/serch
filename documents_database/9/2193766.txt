it blooms from may to september and produces vert sweetly scented white cream yellow pink flowers in racemes in the leaf axils and upper branchlets the leaves are short terete and sharply pointed divided into many segments the ovoid fruit are smooth to roughish and warty long by tapering to two short beaks hakea lissocarpha was first formally described in 1830 by robert brown it is named from the greek lissos smooth and carphos dry grass referring to the bracts surrounding the flower bud a variable and common species widespread from coast of northampton to israelite bay grows in heath or woodland on sand loam lateritic gravel or limestone requires a sunny or semi shaded aspect with good drainage may be used for hedging and wildlife habit and is drought and frost tolerant hakea lissocarpha is classified as not threatened by the western australian government
