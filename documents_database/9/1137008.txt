the model 54 was a conventional biplane design with a highly streamlined fuselage similar in outline to the curtiss eagle but of considerably smaller proportions the wings were fitted with a variety of high lift devices including automatic leading edge slots on the upper wing flaps that extended along the entire span of the upper wing and floating ailerons on the lower wing that in the absence of pilot input automatically adjusted themselves parallel to the airflow over the wing the combination of these devices gave the tanager a stall speed of just 31 mph 50 km h and allowed it to land in only 90 ft 27 m only the tanager and the handley page gugnunc passed the qualifying round of the competition and ultimately the tanager was to beat its rival by only one point to claim the 100 000 20 000 prize the gugnunc failing to achieve a minimum speed below 38 mph even before the competition was decided however handley page was suing curtiss for the unlicensed use of the leading edge slot curtiss claimed they were using the slots experimentally and would apply for a license for any commercial use curtiss counter sued handley page
