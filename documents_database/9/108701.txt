like most old english poetry it is written in alliterative verse rood is from the old english word r d pole or more specifically crucifix preserved in the 10th century vercelli book the poem may be as old as the 8th century ruthwell cross and is considered one of the oldest works of old english literature the poem is set up with the narrator having a dream in this dream or vision he is speaking to the cross on which jesus was crucified the poem itself is divided up into three separate sections the first part ll 1 27 the second part ll 28 121 and the third part ll 122 156 in section one the narrator has a vision of the cross initially when the dreamer sees the cross he notes how it is covered with gems he is aware of how wretched he is compared to how glorious the tree is however he comes to see that amidst the beautiful stones it is stained with blood in section two the cross shares its account of jesus death the crucifixion story is told from the perspective of the cross it begins with the enemy coming to cut the tree
