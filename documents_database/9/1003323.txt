he was a member of the illinois house of representatives and was also elected from illinois to the united states house of representatives born in vandalia illinois remann attended the common schools of vandalia and the mifflin pennsylvania academy he was graduated from the iron city business college pittsburgh pennsylvania in april 1865 during the civil war he served as corporal in company e one hundred and forty third regiment illinois volunteer infantry he again attended mifflin academy in 1866 and 1867 and was graduated from illinois college at jacksonville in 1868 he returned to vandalia and engaged in mercantile pursuits he served as county supervisor of fayette county and as alderman of vandalia he served as delegate to numerous republican state conventions he served as member of the state house of representatives in 1877 and 1878 remann was elected as a republican to the fifty fourth congress and served from march 4 1895 until his death in vandalia illinois july 14 1895 before the convening of congress he was interred in south hill cemetery
