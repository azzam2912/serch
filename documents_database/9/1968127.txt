the somerset levels or the somerset levels and moors as they are less commonly but more correctly known is a coastal plain and wetland area of central somerset in south west england running south from the mendip hills to the blackdown hills the levels are a low lying area around above mean sea level o d which have been prone to flooding from fresh water and occasional salt water inundations people have attempted to drain the area for hundreds of years in the middle ages the monasteries of glastonbury athelney and muchelney reclaimed and enclosed much of the land drains and artificial rivers have been built and pumping stations installed during december 2013 and january 2014 heavy rainfall led to extensive flooding with over 600 houses and of agricultural land including north moor curry and hay moors and greylake affected the villages of thorney and muchelney were cut off with many houses flooded northmoor green which is more commonly known as moorland was also severely affected flood relief activities included the use of rescue boats and the army high volume pumps were brought in from the netherlands and installed at several points to try to relieve the flooding prince charles
