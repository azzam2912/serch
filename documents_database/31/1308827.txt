the book focuses on historical markers and museums across the united states the book starts on the west coast and moves east a deliberate break from the traditional history found in textbooks which begin with the pilgrims and move westward loewen covers hispanic rather than english discovery and american indian history loewen s book voices two major complaints about historical markers in the united states the first and most major deals with historical markers established in the southern united states that attempt to whitewash the history of slavery and the period of reconstruction many of these markers were established between 1890 and 1920 the nadir of american race relations most were placed by organizations with pro confederate agendas and reflect the racism of the early 20th century while some markers have been altered in the last 40 years as a result of civil rights progress many have not especially those at american civil war battle sites and in the south loewen s second major complaint deals with the treatment of native americans who are often neglected and omitted in the telling of united states history the author challenges and corrects many of the inaccurate and eurocentric mistruths spread by historical
