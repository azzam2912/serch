the bakalai have suffered much from the incursions of their neighbors the fang also arrivals from the south east and it may be that they migrated to their present abode under pressure from this people at an earlier date they are keen hunters and were traders in slaves and rubber the slave traffic has been prohibited by the french authorities their women display considerable ingenuity in dressing their hair often taking a whole day to arrange a coiffure the hair is built up on a substructure of clay and a good deal of false hair incorporated a coat of red green or yellow pigment often completes the effect the same colors are used to decorate the hut doors the villages some of which are fortified with palisades are usually very dirty chiefs and rich men own plantations which are situated at some distance from the village and to which their womenfolk are sent in times of war the bakalai of lake isanga cremate their dead those of the upper ogowe throw the bodies into the river with the exception of those killed in war the body of a chief is placed secretly in a hut erected in the depths of
