he was exon an officer rank of the yeomen of the guard from 1689 to 1718 he succeeded his father to grafham after 1708 bigg was returned unopposed as member of parliament for huntingdonshire on the interest of the duke of manchester at the 1715 general election he voted with the administration on all occasions in that parliament he was returned again unopposed in 1722 and in 1727 he voted against the government on the army in 1732 and on the repeal of the septennial act in 1734 he retired from parliament in 1734 bigg died unmarried on 24 march 1748 and left his estates to his sister lucy for life and then to his friend sir john bernard 4th baronet
