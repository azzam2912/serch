the ship foundered after sailing about 1 300 m 1 400 yd into its maiden voyage on 10 august 1628 it fell into obscurity after most of her valuable bronze cannon were salvaged in the 17th century until it was located again in the late 1950s in a busy shipping lane just outside stockholm harbor the ship was salvaged with a largely intact hull in 1961 it was housed in a temporary museum called wasavarvet the vasa shipyard until 1988 and then moved permanently to the vasa museum in the royal national city park in stockholm the ship is one of sweden s most popular tourist attractions and has been seen by over 35 million visitors since 1961 since her recovery vasa has become a widely recognised symbol of the swedish great power period the ship was built on the orders of the king of sweden gustavus adolphus as part of the military expansion he initiated in a war with poland lithuania 1621 1629 it was constructed at the navy yard in stockholm under a contract with private entrepreneurs in 1626 1627 and armed primarily with bronze cannons cast in stockholm specifically for the ship richly decorated as a symbol
