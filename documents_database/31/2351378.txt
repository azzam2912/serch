tibialis flies parasitize many different species of bees by laying their eggs inside the abdomen of the host the larva hatches inside of the host and grows and develops until it takes up the majority of the host s abdomen the host then dies and the larva envelopes itself in a puparium and pupates inside of the corpse after an extended period of pupation the adult emerges from the corpse of the host bee using a specialized inflatable pouch on their head called a ptilinum when a bee becomes parasitized by a p tibialis larva certain behaviors such as induced grave digging and changes in flower preference often occur the adult fly is primarily black with a yellow face and thin white stripes on the abdomen p tibialis is found in north america primarily along the east coast of the united states they can be seen as far north as massachusetts and as south as florida they have also been seen as far west as wisconsin and texas p tibialis can often be found near flowering plants likely due to the presence of host species pollinating nearby plants they have not been seen to prefer specific species of plants and
