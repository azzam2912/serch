it was formerly freeware with limitations on commercial use but since 2011 it is available under a free software open source 3 clause bsd license license as a part of the open source webrtc project it is suitable for voip applications streaming audio archival and messaging the algorithm is a version of block independent linear predictive coding with the choice of data frame lengths of 20 and 30 milliseconds the encoded blocks have to be encapsulated in a suitable protocol for transport usually the real time transport protocol rtp ilbc handles lost frames through graceful speech quality degradation lost frames often occur in connection with lost or delayed ip packets ordinary low bitrate codecs exploit dependencies between speech frames which cause errors to propagate when packets are lost or delayed in contrast ilbc encoded speech frames are independent and so this problem will not occur ilbc is defined in rfc 3951 it is one of the codecs used by gizmo5 webrtc ekiga google talk maemo recorder on the nokia n800 n810 polycom ip phone cisco qutecom tuenti yahoo messenger ooma and many others ilbc was submitted to ietf in 2002 and the final specification was published in 2004
