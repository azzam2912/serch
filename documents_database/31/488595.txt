he grew up in patna s imlitala ghetto which was mainly inhabited by dalit hindus and shia muslims his was the only bengali family his father ranjit 1909 1991 was a photographer in patna his mother amita 1916 1982 was from a progressive family of the 19th century bengali renaissance his grandfather laksmikanta roy choudhury was a photographer in kolkata who had been trained by rudyard kipling s father the curator of the lahore museum at the age of three roy choudhury was admitted to a local catholic school and later he was sent to the oriental seminary the school was administered by the brahmo samaj movement a monotheistic religion founded in 1830 in kolkata by ram mohun roy who aimed to purify hinduism and recover the simple worship of the vedas there roy choudhury met student cum librarian namita chakraborty who introduced him to sanskrit and bengali classics all religious activities were banned at the school and roy choudhury has said that his childhood experience made him instinctively secular roy choudhury has proficiency in english hindi bhojpuri and maithili apart from his mother tongue bengali he was influenced though by the shia muslim neighbors who recited ghalib and faiz
