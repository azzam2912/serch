they landed in philadelphia and lived for a time in new castle county in 1720 he purchased 176 acres of land that included much of the then standing village of chalfont from a former land grant founded by william penn in 1730 he finished construction of his house and mill and then purchased over 400 more acres of land in 1745 including much of present day chalfont and new britain during this time butler built a number of gristmills on the sites of original mills such as the funk sawmill and the shellenberger s mill he then acted as the sole justice of the peace in the county and until his death he was the economic and legal power in the area among other important duties he wrote wills and was well known for his extremely sound judgement he was also a devout baptist and helped construct one of the first baptist churches in the area the new britain baptist church after his death in august 1764 his land was parceled to many people most notably george kungle who opened a public house in the 1760s butler lives in local memory through the lending of his name to butler pike
